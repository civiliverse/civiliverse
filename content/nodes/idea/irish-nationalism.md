**卷首引文** — 「你们要在路旁遇见他时，把他孤立起来，如同古时的麻风病人。」——帕内尔1880年恩尼斯演说
*"You must show him on the roadside when you meet him… as if he were the leper of old." — Charles Stewart Parnell, Ennis, 1880*

**定位句** — 一场把「记住那场饥荒」当作政治纲领的运动。
*A movement whose political programme included remembering a famine.*

**历史叙述** — 1848年，青年爱尔兰党在饥荒最深处发动了一场迅速失败的起义，但其成员流亡后成为下一代组织的骨干。1858年，芬尼亚兄弟会（爱尔兰共和兄弟会）在都柏林与纽约同时成立——这一双城结构本身即是饥荒的产物：大规模移民造就了一个有收入、有政治空间、且对故国怀有强烈怨愤的海外社群，其汇款与捐输此后长期支撑岛内的运动。1879年农业歉收与租金压力再起，达维特与帕内尔组建土地同盟，提出「三 F」（公平租金、固定租权、自由转让）。在恩尼斯的演说中，帕内尔提出不以暴力而以社会孤立对付不义的地主与代理人；数周后，梅奥郡的农户以此法对付土地代理人查尔斯·博伊科特，令其无人耕作、无人交易，「boycott」自此进入英语。土地战争最终换来一系列《土地法》，19世纪末的《购地法》使佃户得以贷款购地，地主制逐步瓦解。政治上，自治运动几度受挫，1916年复活节起义与其后的独立战争则通向1922年的建国。
*In 1848, at the depth of the famine, Young Ireland launched a rising that failed quickly; its exiles nonetheless became the cadre of the next generation's organizations. In 1858 the Fenian Brotherhood (the Irish Republican Brotherhood) was founded in Dublin and New York at once—a two-city structure that was itself a product of the famine, since mass emigration had created an overseas community with income, political space, and a strong grievance toward the old country, whose remittances and subscriptions would sustain the movement at home for decades. When harvests failed and rents pressed again in 1879, Michael Davitt and Parnell formed the Land League and demanded the "three Fs": fair rent, fixity of tenure, free sale. In his Ennis speech Parnell proposed meeting unjust landlords and agents not with violence but with social isolation; weeks later tenants in County Mayo applied the method to the land agent Charles Boycott, leaving him without labour or trade, and "boycott" entered the English language. The Land War eventually produced a series of Land Acts, and the purchase acts of the late nineteenth century let tenants borrow to buy their holdings, dissolving landlordism by degrees. Politically, Home Rule was repeatedly frustrated; the Easter Rising of 1916 and the war that followed led to the state of 1922.*

**史论** — 这一条在图谱中的位置有点特别：它是一条从灾祸出发的 inspires 边的终点，而这类边最容易被写得轻飘——仿佛苦难自动转化为觉醒。实情要复杂得多。饥荒的即时效果是瓦解而非动员：最赤贫者死去或离开，剩下的人忙于活命，1848年的起义因此几乎无人响应。真正把饥荒转成政治能量的是**时间与距离**——一代人之后，在纽约与波士顿，饥荒的记忆被重新讲述、组织化、并获得了资金。这提示我们：灾祸与其政治后果之间，隔着记忆的制度化过程，隔着谁在讲述、向谁讲述。另一处值得记的是「boycott」这个词：一个地主代理人的姓氏成了全世界通用的抗争术语。语言里藏着的历史，有时比纪念碑更耐久。
*This node occupies a slightly special place in the graph: it is the target of an inspires edge running out of a disaster, and such edges are easily written glibly, as though suffering converted automatically into awakening. The reality is more complicated. The famine's immediate effect was dissolution, not mobilization: the poorest died or left, and those who remained were occupied with survival, so the rising of 1848 met almost no response. What converted famine into political energy was time and distance—a generation later, in New York and Boston, the memory was retold, organized, and funded. Between a disaster and its political consequences lies the institutionalization of memory: who tells it, and to whom. The word "boycott" is worth noting too. A land agent's surname became the world's common term for a form of struggle. The history stored inside language sometimes outlasts monuments.*
