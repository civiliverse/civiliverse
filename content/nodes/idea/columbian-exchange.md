**卷首引文** — 「一四九二年之后，人类不再生活在同一颗星球的许多个世界里，而是生活在一个世界中。」——综述自克罗斯比《哥伦布大交换》主旨
*"After 1492 humanity ceased to live in many worlds on one planet, and began to live in one." — summarized from Crosby (1972)*

**定位句** — 一场没有人策划、也没有人能叫停的全球生物重组。
*A worldwide biological reorganization that no one planned and no one could call off.*

**历史叙述** — 1492年之后的三个世纪里，两个此前几乎隔绝的生物区系被强行接通。自美洲东传的作物改变了旧大陆的食谱与人口曲线：马铃薯适应北欧的冷湿土壤，在爱尔兰、德意志与俄国成为主粮；玉米与番薯耐旱耐瘠，进入中国山地与非洲，明清人口的增长与之相关（其权重仍有争论）；番茄、辣椒、可可、烟草则改写了地中海、南亚与东亚的饮食与嗜好。反向而来的则是小麦、水稻、甘蔗、咖啡与欧亚的牲畜——马匹进入北美大平原，使原住民的狩猎与战争形态在一个世纪内彻底改变；牛羊的蹄与齿改造了草原。最沉重的一支是病原：天花、麻疹、斑疹伤寒、流感在毫无免疫经验的人群中连年横扫，与战争、奴役、强制迁徙及生计体系的崩坏叠加，造成美洲原住民人口在一个半世纪内的大幅锐减（接触前人口的估计本身分歧极大，故降幅无法给出确数）。此外还有强制迁移的人群：跨大西洋奴隶贸易在同一网络中运行，把西非的人口与作物一并带入美洲。
*In the three centuries after 1492 two long-separated biotas were forcibly joined. Crops moving east from the Americas altered Old World diets and population curves: the potato suited the cold, wet soils of northern Europe and became a staple in Ireland, the German lands, and Russia; maize and sweet potato, tolerant of drought and poor soils, entered the uplands of China and much of Africa, and the demographic growth of the Ming and Qing is associated with them (with the weighting still disputed); tomato, chili, cacao, and tobacco rewrote the cuisines and habits of the Mediterranean, South Asia, and East Asia. Westward went wheat, rice, sugar, coffee, and Eurasian livestock—horses reached the North American plains and transformed Indigenous hunting and warfare within a century, while the hooves and teeth of cattle and sheep remade grasslands. The heaviest strand was pathogenic: smallpox, measles, typhus, and influenza swept year after year through populations with no prior exposure and, compounded by war, enslavement, forced relocation, and the collapse of subsistence systems, brought a drastic decline in Native American populations within a century and a half (pre-contact population estimates themselves diverge widely, so no precise proportion can be given). And there were people moved by force: the transatlantic slave trade ran on the same network, carrying West African populations and crops together into the Americas.*

**史论** — 克罗斯比这一概念的力量，在于它把征服史从纯粹的政治军事叙事中拆开，让生物学入场：西班牙人少而胜多，火器与马匹固然有用，但真正清空了对手的是病原体——这不是为征服辩护（疾病之外还有屠杀、奴役与制度性剥夺），而是要求解释必须完整。同时它逼出一个不对称：**交换是双向的，收益却不是。** 旧大陆得到了热量与人口，新大陆得到的是空掉的村庄。用「交换」这个中性词概括这一切，本身就带着需要警惕的平滑。另一层则关于因果的绵长：今日爱尔兰的饥荒、中国山地的开垦、非洲的木薯田、意大利的番茄酱，都是同一次生物重组的余波。技术史常聚焦器物，而这一条提醒我们，**移动一株植物，有时比发明一台机器改变了更多的人。**
*The power of Crosby's concept is that it prised the history of conquest away from a purely political-military narrative and let biology in: the Spanish were few and prevailed, and while firearms and horses helped, what emptied the field before them was pathogens. This is not a defence of conquest—beyond disease there were massacre, enslavement, and institutional dispossession—but a demand that explanation be complete. It also forces an asymmetry into view: the exchange ran both ways, the benefits did not. The Old World gained calories and population; the New World gained emptied villages. To sum all this under the neutral word "exchange" carries a smoothness that deserves suspicion. A further layer concerns the length of causation: the Irish famine, the opening of Chinese uplands, the cassava fields of Africa, and the tomato sauce of Italy are aftershocks of one biological reorganization. The history of technology tends to focus on artefacts; this entry is a reminder that moving a single plant has sometimes changed more lives than building a machine.*
