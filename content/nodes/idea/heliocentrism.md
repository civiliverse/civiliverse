**卷首引文** — 「于万象之中央，太阳端坐……仿佛坐于王座，统御着环绕它的群星家族。」——哥白尼《天体运行论》卷一（1543）
*"In the middle of all sits the Sun enthroned… ruling the family of planets that wheel around him." — Copernicus, De revolutionibus, 1543*

**定位句** — 一个早在两千年前就有人提出、却被搁置了十八个世纪的想法：也许，动的是我们。
*An idea proposed two thousand years earlier and then shelved for eighteen centuries: perhaps it is we who move.*

**历史叙述** — 太阳居中的猜想并非始于哥白尼。约前270年，希腊的阿里斯塔克斯已提出地球绕日、并自转，但在亚里士多德—托勒密地心体系的权威与「若地动何以感觉不到」的常识面前，此说湮没千年。1543年，波兰教士哥白尼在临终付梓的《天体运行论》中系统重建日心模型，以之解释行星的逆行等现象。近世研究发现，哥白尼所用的若干数学工具（如「图西偶」）与伊斯兰马拉盖学派天文学家（图西、伊本·沙提尔）的模型高度吻合，其学说实承接了阿拉伯天文的推进。此后开普勒以第谷的精密观测定出椭圆轨道，伽利略用望远镜看见金星盈亏与木星卫星，为日心提供实证，也因此在1633年遭罗马宗教裁判所审判、被迫弃说。直到牛顿的万有引力，日心体系才获得坚实的物理根基。
*The conjecture of a central Sun did not begin with Copernicus. Around 270 BCE the Greek Aristarchus proposed that the Earth circles and spins about the Sun, but before the authority of the Aristotelian-Ptolemaic geocentric system and the common sense of "if the Earth moved, why do we not feel it," the idea lay buried for a thousand years. In 1543 the Polish canon Copernicus, in De revolutionibus, published on his deathbed, systematically rebuilt the heliocentric model to explain such phenomena as planetary retrogradation. Recent scholarship finds that several of Copernicus's mathematical tools (such as the "Tusi couple") match closely the models of the Islamic Maragha-school astronomers (al-Tusi, Ibn al-Shatir), so that his doctrine in fact inherited the advances of Arab astronomy. Thereafter Kepler, from Tycho's precise observations, set elliptical orbits; Galileo, with the telescope, saw the phases of Venus and the moons of Jupiter, furnishing evidence for heliocentrism—and for it was tried by the Roman Inquisition in 1633 and forced to recant. Only with Newton's universal gravitation did the heliocentric system win a solid physical footing.*

**史论** — 日心说最耐人寻味的，是阿里斯塔克斯的遭遇：正确的想法早了一千八百年提出，却因缺乏配套的证据（望远镜）、力学（惯性）与数学（椭圆），无人问津。这与活字印刷的命运遥相呼应——**技术上或观念上的「先到」，从不等于历史上的「先赢」**；一个想法能否落地生根，取决于它降临时周遭是否已备好接纳它的整个条件网络。日心说也是破除辉格史观的绝佳案例：哥白尼本人其实相当保守，仍执着于完美的圆周运动、仍用本轮，是个「不情愿的革命者」；而这场「革命」的数学血液里，流着伊斯兰天文的贡献。把日心说奉为「欧洲理性突然睁眼」的神话，既冤枉了阿里斯塔克斯，也抹去了马拉盖。它更像一条长河的入海处，而非凭空涌出的泉眼。
*What most repays thought in heliocentrism is the fate of Aristarchus: a correct idea proposed eighteen centuries too early, ignored for want of the evidence (the telescope), the mechanics (inertia), and the mathematics (the ellipse) to support it. This echoes the fate of movable type—being "first" in technology or in idea is never the same as being "first" to win in history; whether an idea takes root depends on whether the whole network of conditions to receive it was ready when it arrived. Heliocentrism is also a fine case against Whig history: Copernicus himself was quite conservative, still wedded to perfect circular motion and still using epicycles, a "reluctant revolutionary"; and in the mathematical blood of this "revolution" ran the contributions of Islamic astronomy. To enshrine heliocentrism as the myth of "European reason suddenly opening its eyes" wrongs Aristarchus and erases Maragha alike. It is more the estuary of a long river than a spring welling from nowhere.*
