**卷首引文** — 「三代低能已经够了。」——美国最高法院大法官霍姆斯，巴克诉贝尔案判词（1927）
*"Three generations of imbeciles are enough." — Justice Oliver Wendell Holmes Jr., Buck v. Bell (1927)*

**定位句** — 二十世纪前期，主张以国家之力管理谁可以生育，在大多数发达国家不是极端立场，而是学界与政界的通行见解。
*In the first half of the twentieth century, holding that the state should govern who may reproduce was not an extreme position in most developed countries; it was the received opinion of learned and political society alike.*

**历史叙述** — 高尔顿于 1883 年造出「优生学」一词，主张人类的才能既然可以统计，就可以经由婚配与生育加以改良。此说在孟德尔遗传学重新被发现后声势大盛：伦敦大学设优生学讲席，美国有冷泉港的优生记录办公室，国际优生学大会自 1912 年起定期召开，与会者中有当时最负盛名的生物学家、经济学家与公共卫生官员。它在政策上的落点因国而异。美国走得最远也最早：1907 年印第安纳州率先立法许可对「不适者」强制绝育，此后逾三十州跟进；1927 年最高法院在巴克诉贝尔案中以八比一维持弗吉尼亚州的绝育法，判词出自素有自由派声誉的霍姆斯大法官之手；二十世纪内被依此类法律强制绝育者约六万人，其中黑人、贫民与被收容的女性比例远高于其人口占比。北欧诸国的立法在 1930 年代前后出台，其论证框架混合了优生与福利国家的成本考量，部分法律迟至 1970 年代才废止。德国 1933 年的《遗传病后代预防法》直接参照了美国加州的立法经验；1939 年起的「T4 行动」把此一话语推至杀害——由医生主持、以医学鉴定表格筛选对象，其人员与技术在 1941 年秋被转调至波兰东部的灭绝营。战后，「优生学」一词在学界近乎绝迹，多数学会改名或解散；但强制绝育的赔偿与道歉，在美国、瑞典、日本等国要到 1990 年代乃至二十一世纪才陆续开始。
*Galton coined "eugenics" in 1883, arguing that since human ability could be counted it could be improved by marriage and generation. The rediscovery of Mendelian inheritance gave the programme great impetus: a chair of eugenics was founded at University College London, the Eugenics Record Office at Cold Spring Harbor in the United States, and international congresses met from 1912, attended by some of the most eminent biologists, economists, and public-health officials of the day. Its policy expression varied by country. The United States went earliest and furthest: Indiana legislated in 1907 for the compulsory sterilization of the "unfit," more than thirty states followed, and in 1927 the Supreme Court upheld Virginia's statute eight to one in Buck v. Bell, in an opinion written by Justice Holmes, a jurist of liberal repute. Some sixty thousand people were sterilized under such laws during the century, with Black Americans, the poor, and institutionalized women represented far above their share of the population. The Nordic statutes came around the 1930s, argued within a framework mixing eugenic claims with the cost calculations of the welfare state; several remained on the books into the 1970s. Germany's Law for the Prevention of Genetically Diseased Offspring of 1933 drew directly on Californian precedent, and from 1939 Aktion T4 carried the discourse to killing—directed by physicians, selecting by medical assessment forms, its personnel and techniques transferred in the autumn of 1941 to the extermination camps in eastern Poland. After the war the word all but vanished from science and most societies renamed themselves or dissolved; compensation and apology for compulsory sterilization, however, began only in the 1990s and after, in the United States, Sweden, and Japan among others.*

**史论** — 优生学在科技史里的位置，不在于它错了，而在于**它错的那种方式**。它不是江湖术士的学说，而是当时最好的科学家用当时最好的方法做出来的东西——皮尔逊与费希尔为它发展的统计工具至今仍在使用，与它有关的期刊经过同行评议，它的支持者名单里有社会改革家、女权主义者与公共卫生的先驱。这意味着「专业共识」「同行评议」「统计显著」这些我们赖以判断可信度的标志，全部可以在一个根本错误的纲领上同时亮起绿灯。它真正的病灶不在方法而在前提：它把「人的价值」偷偷换成了「人的遗传效用」，而这一步换算从未被当作需要论证的命题，只是作为不言自明的背景摆在那里——这与效率至上主义的病理是同一种（见 `efficiency-ideology`）。关于它与纳粹屠杀的关系，须格外分寸：优生学是国际性的，而灭绝政策不是。英国、美国、瑞典都有优生学，且都没有走向灭绝营；从「限制生育」到「系统杀害」之间，隔着一次决定性的政治跳跃，那一跳不是由科学完成的。**因此正确的说法不是「优生学导致了大屠杀」，而是「优生学为大屠杀提供了现成的医学语言与一批愿意执行的专业人员」**——这已经足够沉重，不需要再夸大。最后一层与当下有关：基因筛查与生殖医学今天所提的问题，与优生学当年所提的问题在形式上有相似之处，但有一处决定性的差别——是国家决定，还是当事人决定。这一区别是二十世纪用极高的代价换来的，不宜轻易模糊。
*Eugenics occupies its place in the history of technology not because it was wrong but because of the manner of its wrongness. It was not the doctrine of charlatans but the work of the best scientists of the day using the best methods of the day: the statistical tools Pearson and Fisher developed for it are still in use, its journals were refereed, and its supporters included social reformers, feminists, and pioneers of public health. Which means that professional consensus, peer review, and statistical significance—the marks by which we judge credibility—can all show green at once over a programme wrong at its root. The lesion lay not in the methods but in a premise: it silently exchanged the worth of a person for the hereditary utility of a person, and the exchange was never treated as a proposition requiring argument but set out as self-evident background. The pathology is the same as that of the ideology of efficiency (see efficiency-ideology). On its relation to the Nazi killing, proportion is essential: eugenics was international; extermination policy was not. Britain, the United States, and Sweden all had eugenics and none arrived at the death camps. Between restricting reproduction and systematic killing lies a decisive political leap, and science did not make it. The correct statement is therefore not that eugenics caused the Holocaust but that eugenics supplied it with a ready medical vocabulary and a body of professionals willing to act—which is grave enough without exaggeration. A last layer touches the present. The questions raised by genetic screening and reproductive medicine resemble in form those eugenics raised, with one decisive difference: whether the state decides or the person concerned decides. That distinction was bought at very great cost in the twentieth century and should not be blurred lightly.*

**〔不采之边 · 登记〕**
- `germ-theory —informs→ eugenics`／`eugenics —derives→ germ-theory`（**乙类·史学不成立**）：二者同属十九世纪医学，但优生学的方法学来源是遗传统计（高尔顿—皮尔逊一线）与孟德尔学说，**不是**细菌学。把二者相连会制造一条不存在的学脉。**坚决不连。**
- `eugenics —informs→ research-ethics`（**丙类·语义冗余且方向失真**）：战后研究伦理的直接触发是**纽伦堡医生审判所审的人体实验**，而非优生学本身；该关系已由 `holocaust —inspires→ research-ethics` 承载，自本条另连一条会使读者以为优生学是研究伦理的思想来源，方向恰好反了。**不采。**
- `vaccination —informs→ eugenics`／任何公共卫生技术 → 本条（**丙类·枢纽噪声**）：优生学确曾借公共卫生的组织形式与合法性，但若自每一项公卫技术向本条连边，本条将成为二十世纪医学的枢纽噪声。此关系写在正文（「其论证框架混合了优生与福利国家的成本考量」）。**不采。**
