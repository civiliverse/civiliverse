**卷首引文** — 「过去，人是第一位的；将来，制度必须是第一位的。」——泰勒《科学管理原理》（1911）
*"In the past the man has been first; in the future the system must be first." — F. W. Taylor, The Principles of Scientific Management (1911)*

**定位句** — 一门以秒表为工具的学问，最终改变的不是工时，而是人们判断一件事好坏时所用的那把尺。
*A discipline whose instrument was a stopwatch, and whose lasting change was not to the working day but to the ruler by which people judge whether anything is any good.*

**历史叙述** — 泰勒于 1880 年代在费城米德维尔钢铁厂做工长时，认定工人「磨洋工」是理性行为——按件计酬的工厂一旦发现某人产量高，便下调单价，故人人心照不宣地保留余力。他的解法不是道德劝诫，而是把工作本身拆开：以秒表逐一测定每个动作的时间，剔除多余的，把剩下的重组为「唯一最佳方法」，再据此定出无可争辩的定额。1911 年《科学管理原理》出版，随即成为二十世纪流传最广的管理文本之一。其核心不止于计时。泰勒明言，管理层须把长期存在于工匠头脑与手上的经验知识，收集、归类、制成卡片——知识从此属于制度而非属于人。同一时期，吉尔布雷斯夫妇以摄影分解动作，把「效率」从工厂带进医院手术室与主妇的厨房；进步时代的市政改革者以之重编预算与学制；1920 年代德国的理性化运动、苏联的劳动科学组织运动，各自取走了它的一部分——列宁一面斥泰勒制为「资产阶级剥削的精致化」，一面主张必须学会它。它所到之处，「效率」都从一个可用于比较手段的形容词，变成了一个可以独自站立的目标。
*Working as a foreman at the Midvale Steel plant in Philadelphia in the 1880s, Taylor concluded that soldiering—deliberate underworking—was rational behaviour: in a piece-rate shop, any man whose output rose saw his rate cut, so everyone tacitly held something back. His answer was not exhortation but the dismantling of the work itself: time each motion with a stopwatch, discard the superfluous, recombine the rest into the one best way, and set from it a quota beyond argument. The Principles of Scientific Management appeared in 1911 and became one of the most widely circulated management texts of the century. Its core was not timing alone. Management, Taylor wrote, must gather the experiential knowledge that had always lived in the craftsman's head and hands, classify it, and reduce it to cards—knowledge thereafter belonging to the system rather than to the person. In the same years Frank and Lillian Gilbreth used photography to decompose motion, carrying efficiency from the factory into the operating theatre and the housewife's kitchen; Progressive municipal reformers recast budgets and school timetables by it; the German rationalization movement of the 1920s and the Soviet movement for the scientific organization of labour each took a share—Lenin denouncing Taylorism as a refinement of bourgeois exploitation while insisting it must be learned. Wherever it went, efficiency turned from an adjective for comparing means into a goal able to stand on its own.*

**史论** — 效率之所以危险，恰恰在于它看上去不像一种价值观。说「我主张更快更省」，听上去只是在陈述一个人人都会同意的常识，而非在主张什么；正因如此，它可以不经辩护就取得别的价值必须辩护才能取得的地位。布雷弗曼在《劳动与垄断资本》中指出，泰勒制真正的产物不是效率而是**去技能化**：当构思与执行被制度性地分开，工人失去的不只是自主，还有对自己所做之事的整体理解。鲍曼在《现代性与大屠杀》中把这条线延长：一个把「把事情做好」当作最高指令、而把「所做何事」交给上级的组织，在道德上是空心的——它的每一个环节都可以由正派的人尽职地完成，而整体做成的事无人负责。这是二十世纪最沉重的一项指控，也必须连同它的反驳一起读：批评者指出，鲍曼的解释无法说明东线那约一百五十万次面对面的枪杀——那里没有任何官僚距离可言；把大屠杀归结为现代性的一般属性，也会淡化其中具体的反犹意识形态，而后者是决定性的。本图谱不为这场争论作结。可以说的只是较窄的一句：**效率是一种关于「怎么做」的知识，它对「做什么」这个问题不但不给答案，还会因其自身的说服力而使人忘了这个问题需要答案。**
*Efficiency is dangerous precisely because it does not look like a value. To say "I am for faster and cheaper" sounds like stating a commonplace rather than advocating anything, and for that reason it can occupy without argument a position other values must argue for. In Labor and Monopoly Capital, Harry Braverman argued that Taylorism's real product was not efficiency but deskilling: once conception and execution are institutionally separated, what the worker loses is not only autonomy but any grasp of the whole of what he does. Bauman, in Modernity and the Holocaust, extended the line: an organization whose supreme instruction is to do the job well, and which refers the question of what job to those above, is morally hollow—every link may be discharged conscientiously by decent people while what is made by the whole is no one's responsibility. This is among the gravest indictments of the twentieth century, and it must be read together with its rebuttal: critics observe that the account cannot explain some one and a half million face-to-face shootings in the East, where no bureaucratic distance existed at all, and that reducing the killing to a general property of modernity dilutes the specific antisemitic ideology that was decisive. This graph does not settle the dispute. The narrower thing that can be said is this: efficiency is knowledge about how, which not only gives no answer to the question of what, but by its own persuasiveness makes one forget that the question requires one.*
