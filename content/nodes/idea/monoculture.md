**卷首引文** — 「一个作物品种的统一，正是它的危险所在。」——综述自美国国家科学院《作物的遗传脆弱性》（1972）
*"The uniformity of a crop variety is precisely where its danger lies." — summarized from the U.S. NAS report Genetic Vulnerability of Major Crops (1972)*

**定位句** — 把一整片土地押在同一个品种上，等于让一种病菌拥有一把万能钥匙。
*Betting an entire landscape on one cultivar hands a single pathogen a master key.*

**历史叙述** — 单一栽培随商品农业而兴。种植园经济要求可标准化、可批量交易的产出——甘蔗、棉花、烟草、咖啡；19世纪的机械化与20世纪的化肥、农药、良种进一步奖励整齐划一：整片地同时成熟、同一规格入库、同一套机器作业。代价则在遗传层面。爱尔兰在19世纪上半叶对高产的「卢珀」马铃薯形成高度依赖，全岛所种者多为无性繁殖的同一基因型，1845年致病疫霉登陆后几无抗性可言（灾难的成因还包括土地制度与救济政策，见「爱尔兰大饥荒」）。20世纪中叶，全球香蕉贸易几乎全押在「大麦克」一个品种上，巴拿马病使之在商业上灭绝，产业改种「华蕉」——而新的小种如今又在逼近。1970年，美国玉米因广泛采用同一种细胞质雄性不育系，南方叶枯病一年内损失约15%的产量，直接促成了美国国家科学院《作物的遗传脆弱性》报告与其后的种质资源保护计划。
*Monoculture rose with commodity agriculture. Plantation economies demanded standardizable, bulk-tradable output—sugar, cotton, tobacco, coffee; mechanization in the nineteenth century and fertilizers, pesticides, and improved varieties in the twentieth rewarded uniformity further: a whole field ripening at once, one grade into store, one set of machines across it. The cost falls at the genetic level. In the first half of the nineteenth century Ireland came to depend heavily on the high-yielding Lumper potato, most of the island's crop being a single clonally propagated genotype, so that when Phytophthora infestans arrived in 1845 there was essentially no resistance (the causes of the catastrophe also include land tenure and relief policy—see "the Great Irish Famine"). In the mid-twentieth century the global banana trade rested almost wholly on the Gros Michel cultivar, which Panama disease rendered commercially extinct; the industry switched to Cavendish—and new races are now closing in. In 1970 U.S. maize, widely built on one cytoplasmic male-sterile line, lost roughly fifteen percent of the crop in a single season to southern corn leaf blight, prompting the National Academy of Sciences report Genetic Vulnerability of Major Crops and the germplasm conservation programmes that followed.*

**史论** — 单一栽培是斯科特所说「国家的视角」的农业版本：为了可测量、可征收、可管理，把复杂的在地生态简化成一张整齐的表格。简化确实带来了效率——问题在于，被简化掉的那部分（品种杂糅、间作、地方性知识）恰恰是系统的缓冲垫。图谱把它列为 idea 而非 tech，正因为它首先是一种**组织与思维方式**，其次才是具体做法：机械、化肥与育种都只是执行这套逻辑的手段。它也是「技术—灾祸」回路里最不显眼的一环——爱尔兰饥荒的死因写在病理学上，但它的必要条件写在种植制度上。灾难往往不是某个环节出错，而是整个系统把所有鸡蛋合法地、理性地、逐步地放进了同一个篮子。
*Monoculture is the agricultural version of what James Scott called seeing like a state: to make things measurable, taxable, and administrable, a complex local ecology is simplified into a tidy table. The simplification does deliver efficiency—the trouble is that what gets simplified away (mixed varieties, intercropping, local knowledge) is precisely the system's buffer. The graph lists it as an idea rather than a technology because it is first an organizing and cognitive scheme and only then a set of practices: machinery, fertilizer, and breeding are means of executing that logic. It is also the least conspicuous link in the technology–disaster loop. The cause of death in the Irish famine is written in pathology, but its necessary condition is written in the cropping system. Catastrophe is often not one component failing, but an entire system placing all its eggs, legally, rationally, and by degrees, in one basket.*
