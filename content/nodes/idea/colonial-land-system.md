**卷首引文** — 「佃户但凡改良其地，所增之值即成为向他加租的理由。」——综述自迪冯委员会调查结论（1845）
*"Whatever the tenant added to the value of his holding became the ground for raising his rent." — summarized from the Devon Commission, 1845*

**定位句** — 一套让改良土地变成自找麻烦的制度。
*A system in which improving your land was asking for trouble.*

**历史叙述** — 自都铎与斯图亚特朝的历次「殖民」（plantation）至克伦威尔战后的大规模没收与再分配，爱尔兰的土地所有权在两个世纪内基本转入新教地主之手，其中相当部分长居英格兰，由代理人经营。土地经中间人分层转租，最终到达的佃户多为「随意佃」——无书面租约、无固定期限，地主可随时终止或提租。因此，佃户排水、施肥、建屋所增加的地产价值并不属于自己，反而成为加租的依据；北部部分地区存在「阿尔斯特惯例」（承认租权可售、改良可补偿），全岛其余地方则无此保障。18世纪后期至19世纪前期的人口快速增长与继承时的分地习惯，使地块不断细分，无地或近乎无地的农工只能承租零星「块地」（conacre）种植马铃薯——单位面积热量最高、最耐贫瘠的作物。1845年，迪冯委员会在饥荒爆发前夕提交报告，已指出这套制度对改良的抑制与佃户处境之恶。同年，致病疫霉登陆。
*From the Tudor and Stuart plantations to the mass confiscation and redistribution after the Cromwellian conquest, ownership of Irish land passed within two centuries largely into Protestant hands, a considerable share of them resident in England and operating through agents. Land was sublet in tiers through middlemen, and the tenants at the bottom mostly held at will—no written lease, no fixed term, subject to eviction or an increase of rent at the landlord's discretion. Value that a tenant added by draining, manuring, or building therefore did not belong to him but furnished grounds for raising the rent; parts of the north had the Ulster Custom, which recognized a saleable tenant-right and compensation for improvement, while the rest of the island did not. Rapid population growth from the later eighteenth century, together with the practice of dividing holdings among heirs, fragmented the land further, so that labourers with little or no land rented scraps of conacre and planted potatoes—the crop with the highest caloric yield per unit area and the greatest tolerance of poor ground. In 1845 the Devon Commission, reporting on the eve of the famine, had already set out how the system discouraged improvement and how precarious the tenants were. That same year the blight arrived.*

**史论** — 把爱尔兰大饥荒归因于一种真菌样生物，是技术史里最典型的一类简化。疫霉决定了哪一年出事，土地制度决定了出事时有多少人无处可退。这条节点因此以 causes 边接入饥荒，与「单一栽培」并列为两个源——**灾难的多因结构，在图谱上应当画成多条边，而不是一条粗箭头。** 它同时是一个关于「制度也是技术条件」的例证：租佃规则决定了农民能否投资土地，能否投资又决定了耕作方式与作物选择，作物选择再决定了生态脆弱性。制度不在技术之外，它是技术得以展开或无法展开的那个空间。这条链上任一环节的改变，都会让1845年是另一个样子。
*Attributing the Great Irish Famine to a fungus-like organism is the most typical kind of simplification in the history of technology. The blight determined which year the disaster came; the land system determined how many people had nowhere to fall back to when it did. Hence this node joins the famine by a causes edge, standing beside monoculture as a second source—the multi-causal structure of catastrophe should be drawn on a graph as several edges, not one thick arrow. It is also a demonstration that institutions are technical conditions: tenancy rules decided whether a farmer could invest in his land, investment decided cultivation practice and crop choice, and crop choice decided ecological vulnerability. Institutions do not lie outside technology; they are the space in which technology can or cannot unfold. Change any link in that chain and 1845 looks different.*
