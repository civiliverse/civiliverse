**卷首引文** — 「他们为远行者守夜，如同为死者守夜。」——综述自爱尔兰「美国式守夜」习俗
*"They held a wake for the one who was leaving, as for the dead." — from folklore records of the Irish "American wake"*

**定位句** — 交通技术把一次诀别，慢慢变成了一趟旅程。
*Transport technology slowly turned a farewell into a journey.*

**历史叙述** — 19世纪上半叶的横渡多靠帆船，航程数周至数月，条件恶劣；1847年爱尔兰饥荒高峰期的部分航次死亡率超过两成，「棺材船」之名由此而来。此后技术改变了一切：1850年代起蒸汽船逐步取代帆船，横渡时间缩短至十日左右并趋于稳定；欧洲内陆的铁路把出发者送到港口，美洲的铁路把到达者送往内陆；电报使船期、工价与家书得以快速传递。结果不只是人数的增长，更是迁移**形态**的变化：不再是孤注一掷的举家出走，而是先由一人前往、寄回船票与汇款、再接来下一人的「链式迁移」；不再是有去无回，19世纪末部分族群的返乡率相当可观。爱尔兰的情形最为极端——饥荒的十年间约有二百万人离开，此后数十年持续外流，以至于出生于爱尔兰者有近半生活在海外，汇款成为岛内许多家庭的主要收入来源。同一时期，德意志、斯堪的纳维亚、意大利与东欧的移民潮相继涌起，把欧洲的人口压力、美洲的土地与工业需求接成了一个跨洋系统。
*In the first half of the nineteenth century crossings were made under sail, taking weeks to months in wretched conditions; on some 1847 sailings at the height of the Irish famine mortality exceeded twenty percent, whence the name "coffin ships." Then technology changed everything. From the 1850s steamships progressively displaced sail, cutting the crossing to about ten days and making it predictable; railways in the European interior carried people to the ports and American railways carried them inland; the telegraph moved sailing schedules, wage rates, and family letters quickly. The result was not only greater numbers but a change in the form of migration: no longer a whole family staking everything on one departure, but chain migration—one person going first, sending back a prepaid ticket and remittances, then bringing the next; and no longer necessarily one-way, with substantial return rates for some groups by the century's end. Ireland is the extreme case: some two million left during the famine decade and outflow continued for decades after, until nearly half of all Irish-born people lived abroad and remittances were the principal income of many households at home. Over the same period German, Scandinavian, Italian, and East European migrations followed one another, joining Europe's demographic pressure to America's land and industrial demand in a single transoceanic system.*

**史论** — 移民史常被写成推力与拉力的经济学，而技术在其中往往只作背景板。事实上，交通与通信技术改变的是这件事的**性质**：帆船时代的移民是一次不可逆的赌博，蒸汽船与电报时代的移民则是一项可以分期、可以试错、可以往返的家庭策略。这一转变的社会后果极深——它使离散社群得以维持与故乡的持续联系，从而既能寄回改变家乡经济的汇款，也能寄回改变家乡政治的资金（见「爱尔兰民族主义」）。这也是本图谱把它列为 idea 而非某种运输技术的理由：真正的对象不是船，而是**「离开」这件事被重新组织的方式**。至于那个「美国式守夜」的习俗，它恰好标记了转变的临界点：当人们仍以送葬之礼送别活人时，技术还没来得及把大西洋变窄。
*Migration history is often written as the economics of push and pull, with technology as backdrop. In fact transport and communication changed the nature of the thing: under sail, emigration was an irreversible gamble; under steam and telegraph it became a family strategy that could be staged, revised, and reversed. The social consequences run deep—diaspora communities could maintain continuous contact with home, and so send back both the remittances that changed a home economy and the funds that changed its politics (see "Irish Nationalism"). This is why the graph files it as an idea rather than a transport technology: the object is not the ship but the reorganization of what it means to leave. And the custom of the American wake marks the threshold precisely: while people were still burying the living with the rites of the dead, technology had not yet narrowed the Atlantic.*
