**卷首引文** — 「人作为自然的仆役与诠释者，其所能为、所能知，以他观察到的自然秩序为限……」——培根《新工具》箴言一（1620）
*"Man, being the servant and interpreter of Nature, can do and understand so much and so much only as he has observed of the course of Nature…" — Francis Bacon, Novum Organum, Aphorism I (1620)*

**定位句** — 「科学方法」不是某天被发明出来的一套步骤，而是一群人慢慢学会的一种怀疑自己的纪律。
*The "scientific method" was not a set of steps invented one day, but a discipline of doubting oneself that many people slowly learned.*

**历史叙述** — 近代早期，欧洲逐渐凝聚出一套探究自然的新规范。弗朗西斯·培根在《新工具》(1620) 中主张以系统观察与归纳取代亚里士多德的演绎独尊，并强调用实验「拷问自然」；伽利略把数学与受控实验结合，以斜面、摆与望远镜检验假说，示范了「测量—推算—验证」的路数；笛卡尔则倡演绎与怀疑之法。三者取向各异，却共同把「诉诸经验证据、可重复检验、并容许被推翻」立为知识的准绳。这套规范并非凭空而来：它上承中世纪伊斯兰学者海什木（Ibn al-Haytham）在光学中已相当自觉的实验取径，也承接经院学关于论证与因果的长期训练。到17世纪下半叶，皇家学会等机构把它制度化——方法从个人的思想主张，变成可共享、可传承的共同体规范。
*In the early-modern period Europe gradually consolidated a new set of norms for inquiring into nature. Francis Bacon, in the Novum Organum (1620), urged systematic observation and induction in place of the sovereignty of Aristotelian deduction, and stressed "putting nature to the question" by experiment; Galileo joined mathematics to controlled experiment, testing hypotheses with inclined planes, pendulums, and the telescope, and modelled a path of measure–infer–verify; Descartes pressed the way of deduction and doubt. Different in stance, together they set appeal to empirical evidence, repeatable testing, and openness to refutation as the standard of knowledge. These norms did not come from nowhere: they built on the already quite self-conscious experimental approach of the medieval Islamic scholar Ibn al-Haytham in optics, and on the long Scholastic training in argument and causation. By the later 17th century, institutions such as the Royal Society made the method communal—turning it from a private intellectual claim into a shared, inheritable norm.*

**史论** — 关于科学方法，最该破除的是两重神话。其一是「唯一方法」的神话：仿佛存在一套放之四海的固定步骤，照做即出科学。科学哲学（波普尔的可证伪、库恩的范式、直到费耶阿本德「怎么都行」的挑衅）早已表明，真实的科学远比任何教科书流程图芜杂多歧——本条因此宁可把它写成「一套逐渐凝聚、仍在争议的规范」，而非一份操作手册。其二是「欧洲独创」的神话：把科学方法说成文艺复兴欧洲凭空的天才闪现，抹去了海什木的实验光学、经院学的因果训练、乃至更早的先声。图谱因此以一条 disputed 的 inspires 边，让「光学（海什木）」接向科学方法，郑重记下这份非欧洲的血脉。方法的要害，或许不在任何一条具体规程，而在一种反常识的**自我怀疑的纪律**：把「我可能错」制度化，是人类最不自然、也最了不起的发明之一。
*On the scientific method, two myths most need dispelling. The first is the myth of "the one method": as if a fixed set of universal steps existed, which one need only follow to produce science. The philosophy of science (Popper's falsifiability, Kuhn's paradigms, down to Feyerabend's provocation that "anything goes") has long shown that real science is far messier and more plural than any textbook flowchart—this node therefore prefers to write it as "a gradually consolidating, still-contested set of norms" rather than an operating manual. The second is the myth of "European originality": casting the scientific method as the Renaissance's genius from nothing, erasing Ibn al-Haytham's experimental optics, the Scholastic training in causation, and earlier precursors. The graph therefore joins "optics (Ibn al-Haytham)" to the scientific method by a disputed inspires edge, recording that non-European lineage with due weight. The heart of method may lie not in any specific procedure but in a counterintuitive discipline of self-doubt: to institutionalize "I might be wrong" is one of humanity's least natural and most remarkable inventions.*
