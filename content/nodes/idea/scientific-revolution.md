**卷首引文** — 「并不存在所谓『科学革命』这回事，而本书正是关于它的。」——夏平《科学革命》开卷首句
*"There was no such thing as the Scientific Revolution, and this is a book about it." — Steven Shapin, 1996*

**定位句** — 一场没有人在当时宣布过、后人却给它定了起止年份的革命。
*A revolution no one declared at the time, to which posterity has since assigned start and end dates.*

**历史叙述** — 1543年，哥白尼的《天球运行论》把太阳置于中心，维萨里的《人体构造》以亲手解剖纠正盖伦；此后一个半世纪，变化在数条战线同时推进。天文学上，第谷的精密观测、开普勒的椭圆轨道、伽利略的望远镜观测（月面、木星卫星、金星相位）逐步瓦解了旧宇宙的物理基础。方法上，培根倡导以实验逼问自然，笛卡尔主张以数学与机械论解释万物，波义耳与胡克把空气泵一类仪器变成产生「事实」的装置。制度上，罗马的猞猁学会、佛罗伦萨的实验学会、伦敦的皇家学会（1660）与巴黎的科学院（1666）相继成立，《哲学汇刊》（1665）开创了同行评议与优先权确认的雏形。1687年，牛顿以万有引力与三条运动定律把天上与地上的运动纳入同一套数学，这被视为一个阶段的完成。与此同时，旧的知识仍在场：牛顿本人留下的炼金术与神学手稿，体量超过他的物理学著作。
*In 1543 Copernicus's De revolutionibus placed the sun at the centre and Vesalius's Fabrica corrected Galen from his own dissections; over the following century and a half, change advanced on several fronts at once. In astronomy, Tycho's precise observations, Kepler's elliptical orbits, and Galileo's telescopic findings—the lunar surface, the moons of Jupiter, the phases of Venus—progressively dismantled the physical basis of the old cosmos. In method, Bacon urged the interrogation of nature by experiment, Descartes the explanation of everything by mathematics and mechanism, while Boyle and Hooke turned instruments such as the air pump into machines for producing "matters of fact." In institutions, the Lincei in Rome, the Cimento in Florence, the Royal Society in London (1660), and the Académie in Paris (1666) followed one another, and the Philosophical Transactions (1665) created the germ of peer review and of establishing priority. In 1687 Newton brought celestial and terrestrial motion under one mathematics with universal gravitation and three laws, which is taken as the completion of a phase. All the while the older knowledge remained present: Newton left more manuscript on alchemy and theology than on physics.*

**史论** — 关于这段历史，最有价值的东西之一是史学界对「革命」这个词的持续争吵。夏平那句著名的开场白并非俏皮话，而是一个方法论声明：所谓革命，是后人从连续的过程中截取、命名并赋予意义的产物；当时的人自称为「自然哲学家」，做的事情在他们看来是修复而非推翻古人。连续论者指出，中世纪经院对运动、无穷与实验的讨论已相当深入；断裂论者则强调，仪器、数学化与学会制度的组合确实产生了此前没有的知识生产方式。本图谱采取的立场是：**保留争议本身**——凡通往本节点的边（印刷革命、科学方法、日心说、宗教改革）一律标 disputed，不是因为这些关联不存在，而是因为它们的**权重**仍在争论中。把一个学界未决的问题在图上画成实线箭头，是图谱最容易犯、也最难被发现的错误。
*One of the most valuable things about this period is the discipline's continuing quarrel over the word "revolution." Shapin's famous opening is not a quip but a methodological declaration: a revolution is something posterity cuts out of a continuous process, names, and invests with meaning. The people involved called themselves natural philosophers and understood their work as restoring the ancients rather than overthrowing them. Continuity theorists point out that scholastic discussion of motion, infinity, and experiment had gone a long way; rupture theorists reply that the combination of instruments, mathematization, and corporate institutions did produce a mode of knowledge-making that had not existed. This graph's position is to preserve the dispute: every edge leading into this node—from the printing revolution, the scientific method, heliocentrism, the Reformation—is marked disputed, not because the connections are absent but because their weight is still argued. Drawing an unsettled scholarly question as a solid arrow is the easiest error a graph can make, and the hardest to notice.*
