**卷首引文** — 「先生们，是我们自己把死亡带给了产妇。」——塞麦尔维斯致维也纳同行（1861）
*"Gentlemen, it is we ourselves who carry death to the mothers." — Semmelweis to his Viennese colleagues (1861)*

**定位句** — 一个正确的结论，因为提出得太早、说得太冲、又说不出为什么，被同行否弃了二十年。
*A correct conclusion, put too early, put too harshly, and unable to say why—and so rejected by the profession for twenty years.*

**历史叙述** — 1847 年，维也纳总医院的塞麦尔维斯注意到一件事：由医生与医学生接生的第一产科病区，产褥热死亡率远高于由助产士接生的第二病区。一位同事在解剖时被刀划伤后病死，症状与产褥热相同，他由此推断医生手上带着「尸体颗粒」，从解剖室直接进了产房。他要求所有人以漂白粉液洗手，第一病区的死亡率随即从约百分之十八落到百分之二以下。这个结论正确，代价却是同行的敌意——它等于指控医生是杀死病人的原因，而他既拿不出机理，又以极强烈的措辞坚持，最终失去职位，1865 年死于精神病院。1854 年伦敦霍乱大流行期间，斯诺把病例逐户标在地图上，发现集中于宽街水泵取水范围内；他更以两家供水公司在同一街区交错供水的「自然实验」，比较其用户的霍乱死亡率，得出水源说。此时瘴气说仍是主流，斯诺同样未能说服多数人。真正使学说成立的是实验室：1860 年代巴斯德以曲颈瓶证明肉汤不接触空中微粒便不腐败，否证了自然发生说；随后李斯特把石炭酸引入外科，术后感染大减。1876 年起，科赫在乡村诊所的简陋条件下分离出炭疽杆菌，继而是结核杆菌（1882）与霍乱弧菌（1883），并提出四条法则：病原须在患者体内被发现、可纯培养、接种健康动物可致同病、可自该动物再分离。至此，一种病与一种微生物的对应关系第一次可以被逐一验证。
*In 1847 Ignaz Semmelweis, at the Vienna General Hospital, observed that the first obstetric clinic, staffed by physicians and medical students, had a far higher mortality from puerperal fever than the second, staffed by midwives. When a colleague cut himself during an autopsy and died with the same symptoms, Semmelweis concluded that physicians carried cadaveric particles on their hands from the dissecting room to the delivery room. He required washing in chlorinated lime, and mortality in the first clinic fell from about 18 per cent to under 2. The conclusion was right, and the cost was his colleagues' hostility—it amounted to charging physicians with killing their patients, and he could offer no mechanism while insisting in the strongest terms. He lost his post and died in an asylum in 1865. During the London cholera epidemic of 1854, John Snow plotted cases house by house and found them clustered within the reach of the Broad Street pump; more decisively, he exploited a natural experiment in which two water companies supplied alternate houses in the same streets, and compared cholera mortality among their customers. Miasma remained the majority view, and Snow likewise failed to convince most of his profession. What established the theory was the laboratory. In the 1860s Pasteur's swan-neck flasks showed that broth kept from airborne particles did not putrefy, refuting spontaneous generation; Lister then brought carbolic acid into surgery and post-operative infection fell sharply. From 1876 Koch, working in the poor conditions of a country practice, isolated the anthrax bacillus, then the tubercle bacillus (1882) and the cholera vibrio (1883), and set out four rules: the organism must be found in the diseased, cultured in pure form, produce the disease when inoculated into a healthy animal, and be recovered from that animal. With this, the correspondence between one disease and one micro-organism could for the first time be verified case by case.*

**史论** — 细菌致病说常被当作「科学战胜迷信」的样板故事，但它更有价值的一面是**它为什么花了那么久**。塞麦尔维斯有数据、有对照、有立竿见影的效果，却失败了；科赫有显微镜下的照片、有可重复的分离培养、有一套明确的判准，于是成功了。二者的差别不在证据的强度，而在证据的**形态**：统计上的关联要求人们相信一个看不见的机制，而培养皿里的菌落让人看见了那个机制。这不是说医学界当年不讲理，而是说**一门学科接受什么算作证据，本身是有历史的**；十九世纪中叶的医学尚未把统计学认作自家的工具，塞麦尔维斯的数字对他们而言不是证据而是巧合。第二层是关于瘴气说的公道话。瘴气说在机理上是错的，但它推动的城市清污、下水道建设、通风与住房改良，实际效果相当可观——伦敦的巴泽尔杰特下水道系统是为除臭而建，结果却切断了霍乱的水路。**一个错误的理论可以指导有效的行动，一个正确的理论也可能长期无从落地**；把「有效」直接读作「正确」，或把「错误」直接读作「无用」，都会读错医学史的相当一部分。第三层与本图谱的一条主线有关：疫苗接种早于细菌说约一百年即已有效（见 `vaccination`），这条「做法先于道理」的关系在图上以一条自技术指向理论的边表达——技术并非总是科学的应用，它常常是科学的**问题来源**。
*The germ theory is often told as science defeating superstition, but its more valuable side is why it took so long. Semmelweis had data, a control, and an immediate effect, and failed; Koch had photographs down a microscope, a reproducible isolation and culture, and an explicit criterion, and succeeded. The difference lies not in the strength of the evidence but in its form: a statistical association asks people to believe in a mechanism they cannot see, while a colony on a plate lets them see it. This is not to say the profession was unreasonable, but that what a discipline accepts as evidence has itself a history: mid-nineteenth-century medicine had not yet taken statistics for one of its own instruments, and Semmelweis's numbers were to his colleagues not evidence but coincidence. A second layer is owed in fairness to miasma. Miasma was wrong in mechanism, and the cleansing of cities, the building of sewers, ventilation, and housing reform that it drove were considerably effective—Bazalgette's London sewers were built to abolish a stench and cut the waterborne path of cholera instead. A false theory may guide effective action, and a true one may for a long time find no purchase; to read effective as true, or false as useless, is to misread a good deal of medical history. A third layer touches one of this graph's running themes: inoculation worked for roughly a century before the germ theory existed (see vaccination), and that relation of practice preceding explanation is drawn here as an edge running from the technique to the theory—technology is not always the application of science, and is often the source of its questions.*
