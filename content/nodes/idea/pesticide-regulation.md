**定位句** — 回路的最后一环：被看见的代价，如何变成约束技术的规则。
*The last link of the loop: how a cost, once seen, becomes a rule that constrains the technology.*

**历史叙述** — 对 DDT 的管制，是"代价—回应"回路走向制度化的一段。1972年，美国环保署署长拉克尔肖斯在漫长听证后，取消了 DDT 的绝大部分用途——这是新生的 EPA 最具标志性的裁决之一。随后数十年，管制从一国走向全球：2001年，《关于持久性有机污染物的斯德哥尔摩公约》通过（2004年生效），把 DDT 与其他持久性有机污染物纳入全球削减框架。但公约并未全禁 DDT：考虑到它在疟疾高发区不可替代的媒介防治作用，专门保留了在世界卫生组织指导下的公共卫生豁免。管制因此不是"一刀切"，而是一场在生态风险与疾病负担之间反复权衡的持续谈判。
*The regulation of DDT is a stretch of the cost-and-response loop turning institutional. In 1972, after long hearings, the US EPA administrator William Ruckelshaus cancelled most uses of DDT—one of the young agency's most emblematic rulings. Over the following decades regulation moved from one nation to the globe: in 2001 the Stockholm Convention on Persistent Organic Pollutants was adopted (in force 2004), bringing DDT and other persistent organic pollutants into a global reduction framework. Yet the Convention did not ban DDT outright: recognizing its irreplaceable role in vector control in high-malaria regions, it kept a specific public-health exemption under World Health Organization guidance. Regulation, then, is not a single clean cut but a continuing negotiation, repeatedly weighing ecological risk against disease burden.*

**史论** — 农药管制作为 DDT 回路的**闭合节点**，最能说明本图谱为何要让"代价"成为一等公民：技术、危机、书写、运动、规则——五个节点首尾相衔，构成一条完整的反馈回路，而不是一段"进步"的直线。同时它也拒绝一个过于圆满的结局：斯德哥尔摩公约的疟疾豁免提醒我们，"矫正"本身也带着新的权衡——过严则疟疾夺命，过宽则生态受损。真正的历史教训不是"我们终于禁了 DDT"，而是"我们学会了：任何强力技术都需要一套同样精细的、可持续修订的约束"。这，才是回路闭合处应当留给读者的问题，而非答案。
*As the closing node of the DDT loop, pesticide regulation best shows why this graph makes "cost" a first-class citizen: technology, crisis, book, movement, rule—five nodes linked end to end into a complete feedback loop, not a straight line of "progress." It also refuses too neat an ending: the Convention's malaria exemption reminds us that "correction" itself carries new trade-offs—too strict and malaria kills, too loose and ecosystems suffer. The real historical lesson is not "we finally banned DDT," but "we learned that any powerful technology needs an equally fine, continually revisable set of constraints." That, rather than an answer, is the question the closing of the loop should leave with the reader.*
