**卷首引文** — 「你们竟称这些人为暴民——可正是这些人，构成了你们的军队与舰队。」——拜伦，1812年上议院演说
*"You call these men a mob… but it is the mob that man your navy, and recruit your army." — Lord Byron, House of Lords, 1812*

**定位句** — 今天「卢德分子」是骂人话，意思是害怕新技术；当年他们要的，是新技术别把手艺人的活路一起拆了。
*"Luddite" is now an insult meaning afraid of new technology. What they actually wanted was that the new technology not dismantle their livelihoods along with their trade.*

**历史叙述** — 拿破仑战争末期的英格兰，物价高涨、出口受阻、工价下跌。1811年3月，诺丁汉郡的织袜工开始捣毁宽幅织机——这类机器织出的是廉价的「剪裁货」，即整片织成后剪开缝合的次品袜，既压低了价格，也败坏了行业声誉。抗争随后扩至约克郡：剪绒工是羊毛整理工序中技术最高、工价最好的一类，起绒机与剪绒机直接取代了他们。兰开夏则是手织工对动力织机。行动多在夜间，由托名「卢德将军」的信件预告，目标选择相当克制——同一间工场里，惯用的旧机器往往完好无损，被砸的只是那几台。政府反应严厉：1812年通过《破坏机器法》，将捣毁机器列为死罪；拜伦在上议院发表处女演说反对，未能阻止。军队一度在中部驻扎逾万人，多于当时在伊比利亚半岛的兵力。1813年约克审判后，十余人被绞死，另有多人流放澳洲，运动至1816年基本平息。
*In the England of the late Napoleonic wars, prices were high, exports blocked, and wages falling. In March 1811 stockingers in Nottinghamshire began breaking wide frames—machines that produced cheap "cut-ups," stockings knitted as a sheet and then cut and sewn, which undercut prices and spoiled the trade's reputation. The movement spread to Yorkshire, where croppers, the most skilled and best-paid finishers of woollen cloth, were being displaced outright by gig mills and shearing frames, and to Lancashire, where handloom weavers faced the power loom. Actions came at night, announced by letters signed for "General Ludd," and target selection was notably disciplined: in the same workshop the customary older machines were often left untouched and only the particular frames destroyed. The government's response was severe: the Frame Breaking Act of 1812 made the offence capital, and Byron's maiden speech in the Lords against it failed to stop it. At one point more than ten thousand troops were stationed in the Midlands, more than Britain then had in the Peninsula. After the York trials of 1813 a dozen or more were hanged and others transported to Australia; by 1816 the movement had largely subsided.*

**史论** — 「卢德分子」一词今天的用法，是语义漂移的一个标本：它从一场关于**工价、产品质量与行业惯例**的具体斗争，变成了「反对技术进步」的通名。汤普森的研究恢复了本来面目——捣毁者的选择性正是证据：他们砸的是特定的机器，不是所有机器。真正的争点在于分配与规则：当新机器的收益归于工场主、成本归于被替代的工匠，而既有的行业法规（学徒制、工价条例）又被议会一并废止时，除了砸机器，他们几乎没有别的申诉渠道。把这段历史读成「愚昧对抗进步」，不但冤枉了当事人，也遮蔽了一个至今有效的问题：**技术变革的收益与代价由谁承担，从来不是技术本身决定的。** 本图谱以 inspires 边把蒸汽动力与可更换零件接入本条，正是要让这条「代价的一侧」在图上可见。
*Today's use of "Luddite" is a specimen of semantic drift: from a concrete struggle over wages, product quality, and trade custom to a general name for opposition to technical progress. Thompson's work restored the original picture, and the breakers' selectivity is the evidence: they destroyed particular machines, not machines. The real issue was distribution and rules. When the gains of the new frames went to the masters and the costs to the displaced, while Parliament simultaneously repealed the trade's existing regulation—apprenticeship, wage-setting statutes—there was almost no other channel of appeal left. Reading this history as ignorance against progress wrongs the participants and also conceals a question still live: who bears the gains and who the costs of technical change is never settled by the technology itself. The graph runs inspires edges from steam power and interchangeable parts into this node precisely to make that side of the ledger visible.*
