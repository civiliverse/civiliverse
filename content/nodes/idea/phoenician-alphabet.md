**卷首引文** — 「这些随卡德摩斯而来的腓尼基人……把许多学问带进希腊，其中最要紧的是字母——在我看来，希腊人此前并无文字。」——希罗多德《历史》卷五
*"These Phoenicians who came with Cadmus… introduced into Greece many kinds of learning, and above all letters, which the Greeks, it seems to me, did not have before." — Herodotus, Histories 5.58*

**定位句** — 二十来个字母，让读写第一次不再是抄写员阶层的专利。
*Twenty-odd letters that, for the first time, freed reading and writing from a scribal caste.*

**历史叙述** — 楔形文字、圣书字都有数百上千个符号，非专职书吏难以掌握。约公元前二千纪中叶，在埃及做工的闪米特人借圣书字之形、取其所记词语的首音，造出一套「原始西奈—迦南」辅音符号——一个符号只记一个辅音。腓尼基人把它精简为约22个字母，随其推罗、西顿的商船遍布地中海。希腊人约公元前8世纪借入，并做出关键一步：拨出几个闲置符号专记元音，成为完整的音素字母。由此再生出拉丁字母（经伊特鲁里亚）、阿拉米字母（再生阿拉伯、希伯来、以至印度诸文字之一系）。今日世界绝大多数拼音文字，都可上溯到这套腓尼基辅音表。
*Cuneiform and hieroglyphs ran to hundreds or thousands of signs, mastered only by professional scribes. In the mid-2nd millennium BCE, Semitic workers in Egypt took the shapes of hieroglyphs and the first sound of the words they named to build a "Proto-Sinaitic/Canaanite" set of consonant signs—one sign, one consonant. The Phoenicians pared it to some 22 letters and carried it across the Mediterranean on the ships of Tyre and Sidon. The Greeks borrowed it around the 8th century BCE and took a decisive further step: assigning a few idle signs to vowels, they made a complete phonemic alphabet. From it descended the Latin alphabet (via Etruscan) and the Aramaic (parent in turn of Arabic, Hebrew, and a line of Indic scripts). Almost every alphabetic script in the world today traces back to this Phoenician consonantal set.*

**史论** — 字母表的革命性，不在符号本身，而在**数量**：从上千个降到二十来个，识字的门槛被砸低了一个数量级——读写不再必然是宫廷书吏的专业。图谱把它列为 idea（书写的抽象原理），与作为 tech（物质书写系统）的「楔形文字」、作为书写材料的「纸草」分层。但要克制辉格式的欢呼：常有人称希腊字母才是「真正的字母」，因为它记了元音——这话有几分道理，却也悄悄把「进步」的终点设在了欧洲。更稳妥的说法是：腓尼基辅音表与希腊元音化，是同一条长链上先后的两环，谁也不是「完成态」。字母也和纸、和数字一样，是**踩着商路走**的——它由推罗的货船带向世界，而非某位天才的独创。
*The alphabet's revolution lay not in its signs but in their number: from thousands down to a couple of dozen, the threshold of literacy dropped by an order of magnitude—reading and writing need no longer be the trade of court scribes. The graph lists it as an idea (the abstract principle of writing), layered apart from cuneiform (a material writing system, a tech) and papyrus (a writing material). But restrain the Whiggish cheer: it is often said that the Greek alphabet is the "true" alphabet because it wrote vowels—there is something to this, yet it quietly sets the endpoint of "progress" in Europe. Better to say that the Phoenician consonantal set and the Greek vocalization are two successive links in one long chain, neither a "finished" state. Like paper and like numerals, the alphabet travelled by trade route—carried into the world on the freighters of Tyre, not devised by a single genius.*

**图注**（史料图）— 阿希兰石棺铭文（比布鲁斯，约前1000年），已知最早的较长腓尼基字母铭文之一。二十二个符号，日后长成半个世界的文字。／ *The Ahiram sarcophagus inscription (Byblos, c. 1000 BCE), among the earliest longer texts in the Phoenician alphabet. Twenty-two signs that would grow into the scripts of half the world.*
