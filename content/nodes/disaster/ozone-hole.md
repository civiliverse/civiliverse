**卷首引文** — 「这东西没有毒、不可燃、也不腐蚀——问题恰恰在于它什么都不干，于是一路飘到了平流层。」——综述自莫利纳与罗兰1974年论文的论证思路
*"The stuff is non-toxic, non-flammable, non-corrosive—and that is exactly the problem." — summarized from Molina and Rowland (1974)*

**历史叙述** — 氯氟烃于1930年代问世时，被视为化学工业的模范产品：无毒、不燃、不腐蚀，稳定得几乎不与任何东西反应，因而广泛用作制冷剂、发泡剂与喷雾推进剂。1974年，莫利纳与罗兰在《自然》撰文指出，正是这种稳定性使它们能不受干扰地上升至平流层；在那里，紫外线将其分解出氯原子，而一个氯原子可以催化破坏上万个臭氧分子。理论提出后争议激烈，产业界质疑其为「未经证实的假说」。1985年，英国南极调查局的法曼、加德纳与尚克林报告了哈雷湾观测站的地面数据：南极春季臭氧总量较1970年代下降逾三成。卫星数据随后被重新核查——其中的极低值此前因超出预设阈值而被标记待核，并非如流传所说被电脑「自动丢弃」。空洞既经确认，进程骤然加快：1987年《蒙特利尔议定书》议定分阶段淘汰，其后历次修正不断收紧，最终获全球普遍批准。至21世纪初，大气中受管制物质的浓度开始下降，模型预测南极臭氧层于本世纪六十年代前后恢复至1980年水平。
*When chlorofluorocarbons appeared in the 1930s they were a model product of the chemical industry: non-toxic, non-flammable, non-corrosive, so stable they reacted with almost nothing—hence their wide use as refrigerants, blowing agents, and aerosol propellants. In 1974 Molina and Rowland argued in Nature that this very stability let them rise undisturbed into the stratosphere, where ultraviolet light frees chlorine atoms, and a single chlorine atom can catalytically destroy tens of thousands of ozone molecules. The claim was fiercely contested, with industry calling it an unproven hypothesis. In 1985 Farman, Gardiner, and Shanklin of the British Antarctic Survey reported ground data from Halley Bay: total Antarctic ozone in spring had fallen by more than thirty percent from 1970s values. Satellite records were then re-examined—their extreme low values had been flagged for checking as beyond preset thresholds, not, as the popular account has it, discarded automatically by a computer. Once the hole was confirmed events moved quickly: the Montreal Protocol of 1987 set a phase-out schedule, successive amendments tightened it, and it eventually achieved universal ratification. By the early twenty-first century concentrations of controlled substances had begun to fall, and models project Antarctic ozone returning to 1980 levels around the 2060s.*

**史论** — 在本图谱的灾祸序列里，臭氧层是唯一一条**结局向好**的。值得追问的是为什么。技术上，氯氟烃有可行的替代品，且集中于少数厂商，监管对象清晰；科学上，损害机制单一、可归因、可测量；政治上，紫外线增强与皮肤癌的关联把风险直接落到了发达国家选民身上。四项条件同时具备，才有了那份被反复称颂的议定书——这提醒我们，成功案例的可复制性往往低于人们的期待。另一处教益在1985年那次确认：地面观测站的一组「不可能的数据」之所以能触发全球行动，是因为有人坚持长期驻守、逐年记录一个当时看不出用处的变量。**监测本身是一种基础设施**，它的价值只有在出事的那一刻才被看见。至于常被讲成「电脑丢掉了数据」的故事，事实要平淡些：异常值被标记待核而非丢弃——但流传的版本比事实更受欢迎，这本身也值得记一笔。
*In this graph's sequence of disasters, the ozone layer is the only one that ends well, and it is worth asking why. Technically, CFCs had feasible substitutes and were made by a handful of firms, so the object of regulation was clear. Scientifically, the mechanism of harm was single, attributable, and measurable. Politically, the link between increased ultraviolet and skin cancer landed the risk squarely on voters in wealthy countries. Only with all four conditions present did the much-praised protocol become possible—a reminder that success stories are usually less transferable than people hope. The other lesson lies in the confirmation of 1985: a set of "impossible" readings from a ground station could trigger global action because someone had kept the station staffed and recorded, year after year, a variable of no evident use at the time. Monitoring is itself infrastructure, and its value becomes visible only at the moment something goes wrong. As for the tale that a computer threw the data away, the truth is duller: anomalies were flagged for checking, not discarded—yet the popular version travels better than the fact, which is worth noting too.*
