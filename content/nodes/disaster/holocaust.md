**卷首引文** — 「它发生过，因此它可能再次发生：这是我们必须说出的核心。」——普里莫·莱维《被淹没与被拯救的》（1986）
*"It happened, therefore it can happen again: this is the core of what we have to say." — Primo Levi, The Drowned and the Saved (1986)*

**定位句** — 本图谱收录这一条，是因为科技史必须回答一个它无法回避的问题：现代技术与组织能力，也曾被用于系统地杀人。
*This graph includes this entry because the history of technology must answer a question it cannot evade: modern technical and organizational capacity has also been used to kill systematically.*

**历史叙述** — 1933年后，纳粹德国以法律与行政手段逐步剥夺犹太人的公民权、财产与生计，其执行依托于现代国家已有的登记与统计体系：户籍、税册、职业登记、教会婚丧记录被交叉比对以确定「谁是犹太人」，部分环节使用了霍勒瑞斯式穿孔卡制表设备。1939年起，「T4行动」以「不值得活的生命」为名，对精神病院与残障机构中的病患实施系统杀害，约二十万至三十万人遇害；该计划由医生主持、以医学鉴定表格筛选对象，并首次使用一氧化碳毒气室与伪造死亡证明的制度化流程。1941年秋，T4 的核心人员与设备被转调至波兰东部，用于「莱茵哈德行动」的三座灭绝营——这是纳粹屠杀史上技术与人员承继关系最明确的一环。与此同时，别动队在东线以枪杀方式屠杀约一百五十万人。奥斯维辛等营地则将强制劳动、毒气（齐克隆B，原为仓储熏蒸用杀虫剂）与工业规模的焚尸设备结合，托普夫父子公司等承包商为焚尸炉设计申请了专利并竞争订单。整个过程依赖铁路调度：帝国铁路按人头与里程向执行机构开具账单，运输被作为普通的运费业务处理。
*After 1933 Nazi Germany stripped Jews of civil rights, property, and livelihood through law and administration, executed by means of the modern state's existing apparatus of registration and statistics: residence records, tax rolls, occupational registers, and church records of marriage and death were cross-referenced to determine "who is a Jew," and Hollerith punched-card tabulating equipment was used at some stages. From 1939 "Aktion T4," in the name of "life unworthy of life," systematically killed patients in psychiatric and disability institutions—some two to three hundred thousand people. The programme was directed by physicians, selected victims by medical assessment forms, and introduced both carbon-monoxide gas chambers and an institutionalized procedure of falsified death certificates. In the autumn of 1941 T4's core personnel and equipment were transferred to eastern Poland for the three extermination camps of Operation Reinhard—the clearest line of technical and personnel continuity in the history of the killing. In the same period the Einsatzgruppen shot approximately one and a half million people in the East. Camps such as Auschwitz combined forced labour, gas (Zyklon B, originally a fumigant insecticide for storage), and cremation installations at industrial scale, for which contractors including Topf & Söhne designed furnaces, filed patents, and competed for orders. The whole depended on railway scheduling: the Reichsbahn billed the executing agencies per head and per kilometre, handling the transports as ordinary freight business.*

**史论** — 关于这段历史与「现代性」的关系，有两种影响极大的解释，也各有其重要的批评者，本条并陈而不选边。鲍曼在《现代性与大屠杀》中主张，屠杀不是文明的中断而是现代性的产物：劳动分工使每个环节的执行者只面对一份文件、一段铁轨、一张订单，道德责任在链条中被稀释；官僚理性把「解决问题」的技术态度施加于人。阿伦特在《艾希曼在耶路撒冷》中以「平庸之恶」描述一个不思考、只求把工作做好的执行者形象。两说都触及了要害，也都受到实证史学的有力修正：施坦格奈特依据艾希曼战后在阿根廷的大量录音证明，他并非无思想的办事员，而是一个自觉的、以其反犹信念自豪的行动者——「平庸」在很大程度上是他在法庭上的自我塑造；对鲍曼的批评则指出，「官僚距离」无法解释东线那一百五十万次面对面的枪杀，而将屠杀归结为现代性的一般属性，会淡化其中具体的反犹意识形态内容。这场争论本身，是科技史必须记住的部分：**把大屠杀说成「技术使然」与说成「与技术无关」，是同样危险的两种简化。** 技术不曾要求任何人这样做，但它确实提供了规模、速度与那种把人当作待处理事项的操作语法；而那些设计焚尸炉、编排车次、维护表格的人，也从未被技术剥夺过拒绝的能力。
*Two interpretations of this history's relation to "modernity" have been enormously influential, and each has significant critics; this entry sets them side by side without choosing. In Modernity and the Holocaust, Bauman argued that the killing was not an interruption of civilization but a product of modernity: the division of labour left each participant facing only a document, a stretch of track, an order, so that moral responsibility was diluted along the chain, while bureaucratic rationality applied to human beings the technical attitude of problem-solving. In Eichmann in Jerusalem, Arendt described with "the banality of evil" a functionary who did not think and only wished to do his job well. Both touch something essential, and both have been substantially revised by empirical historians: from the extensive recordings Eichmann made in Argentina, Bettina Stangneth showed that he was no thoughtless clerk but a self-aware actor proud of his antisemitic convictions—the "banality" was in large part a self-presentation constructed for the court. Critics of Bauman note that bureaucratic distance cannot account for the million and a half face-to-face shootings in the East, and that reducing the killing to a general property of modernity dilutes the specific antisemitic ideological content within it. The dispute itself is part of what the history of technology must remember: to say the Holocaust was caused by technology and to say it had nothing to do with technology are equally dangerous simplifications. Technology required nothing of anyone, but it did supply scale, speed, and an operational grammar in which persons appear as items to be processed—and those who designed the furnaces, arranged the timetables, and maintained the forms were never deprived by technology of the capacity to refuse.*
