**卷首引文** — 「湖畔苔草已枯萎，不闻鸟鸣。」——济慈《无情的妖女》；卡森取其句为《寂静的春天》题词
*"The sedge is wither'd from the lake, / And no birds sing." — John Keats, "La Belle Dame sans Merci"; taken by Carson as the epigraph of Silent Spring*

**定位句** — 春天何以沉默：一种杀虫剂如何沿着食物链，一路杀到了天空。
*How a spring falls silent: how one insecticide climbed the food chain all the way to the sky.*

**历史叙述** — DDT 不易降解、易溶于脂肪，因而不会随代谢排出，而是在生物体内蓄积。当它沿食物链上行——浮游生物、昆虫、小鱼、小鸟、猛禽——每一级的浓度都成倍放大，这一过程称为生物富集（biomagnification）。在白头海雕、游隼、褐鹈鹕、鱼鹰等顶级捕食者体内，DDT 的代谢物 DDE 干扰钙代谢，使蛋壳变薄、易碎，亲鸟孵卵时压碎己卵，繁殖率骤降，多个物种在1950至60年代濒临崩溃。1962年，蕾切尔·卡森在《寂静的春天》中把这幅"没有鸟鸣的春天"图景呈于公众。1972年美国禁用 DDT 农业用途后，上述猛禽种群在其后数十年间逐步恢复——白头海雕的回归成为生态修复的标志。
*DDT resists breakdown and dissolves in fat, so instead of being excreted it accumulates in living tissue. As it moved up the food chain—plankton, insects, small fish, small birds, birds of prey—its concentration multiplied at each step, a process called biomagnification. In apex predators such as the bald eagle, peregrine falcon, brown pelican, and osprey, DDT's metabolite DDE disrupted calcium metabolism, thinning eggshells until incubating parents crushed their own eggs; reproduction collapsed, and several species approached the brink in the 1950s–60s. In 1962 Rachel Carson set this vision of a "spring without birdsong" before the public in Silent Spring. After the US banned DDT's agricultural use in 1972, these raptor populations recovered over the following decades—the return of the bald eagle became a symbol of ecological repair.*

**史论** — DDT 生态危机之所以是科技史的一等公民，而非环境史的脚注，正因为它揭示了一个此前少有人算的结构：一种技术的代价，可以既不立即、也不局部，而是**沿着生态系统的隐秘管道，延时、放大、远距离地显现**。农人喷洒的是农田，倒下的却是几百公里外食物链顶端的鹰。这一"看不见的因果链"本身，是20世纪生态学与毒理学的一项认识论跃迁——它把"污染"从烟囱口的可见黑烟，扩展为渗入整个生命网络的隐形负债。它以 inspires 边引出《寂静的春天》与其后的环保运动、农药管制，构成 causes→inspires 的完整回路：代价被看见，才可能被矫正。
*The DDT ecological crisis belongs among the first-class citizens of the history of technology, not as a footnote to environmental history, because it revealed a structure few had reckoned with: the cost of a technology can be neither immediate nor local, but can surface—delayed, magnified, and far away—through the hidden conduits of an ecosystem. A farmer sprays a field, and an eagle falls at the top of a food chain hundreds of kilometers away. That "invisible chain of causation" was itself an epistemic leap in 20th-century ecology and toxicology, expanding "pollution" from the visible black smoke at a chimney's mouth to an invisible debt seeping through the whole web of life. Through inspires edges it gives rise to Silent Spring and the environmental movement and pesticide regulation that followed—completing a causes→inspires loop: only once a cost is seen can it be corrected.*
