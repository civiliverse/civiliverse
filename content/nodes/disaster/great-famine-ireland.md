**卷首引文** — 「全能的上帝固然降下了马铃薯疫病，但制造饥荒的是英国人。」——约翰·米切尔《爱尔兰的最后征服（也许）》（1861）
*"The Almighty, indeed, sent the potato blight, but the English created the famine." — John Mitchel, The Last Conquest of Ireland (Perhaps), 1861*

**定位句** — 一场常被称作"天灾"、却最需要被拆穿这一称呼的灾难。
*A catastrophe often called an "act of God"—and one that most needs that label taken apart.*

**历史叙述** — 1845年，致病疫霉（Phytophthora infestans）随进口马铃薯传入爱尔兰，连年摧毁薯作。当时爱尔兰约三分之一人口——尤以贫苦的佃农与雇工——几乎全赖单一而高产的"卢珀"（Lumper）马铃薯为生，这种单一栽培使整个社会暴露在一场病害之下。饥荒随之而来，并伴以斑疹伤寒、痢疾等"饥荒热"。至1852年，约一百万人死于饥饿与疾病，另有约一至二百万人漂洋逃荒。爱尔兰人口从1841年普查的约八百二十万（1845年饥荒前的峰值估计约八百五十万）降至1851年普查的约六百五十万——以普查计减少约五分之一，以饥荒前峰值计则近四分之一。其间，粮食与牲畜仍在从爱尔兰输出——这一事实的净热量意义虽有争论，却成为后世质问的焦点。英国政府的救济受自由放任经济学与"天意"论支配；主管救济的财政部助理次官查尔斯·特雷维廉，甚至视饥荒为上天矫正人口的手段。
*From 1845, the blight Phytophthora infestans arrived with imported potatoes and destroyed Ireland's crop for successive years. About a third of Ireland's people—above all the poor cottiers and labourers—lived almost wholly on the single, high-yielding "Lumper" potato, and this monoculture exposed a whole society to one disease. Famine followed, with "famine fevers" of typhus and dysentery. By 1852 roughly a million had died of hunger and disease, and one to two million more had fled overseas. Ireland's population fell from about 8.18 million at the 1841 census (a pre-famine peak estimated near 8.5 million in 1845) to some 6.5 million at the 1851 census—a fall of roughly a fifth against the census, or nearly a quarter against the pre-famine peak. Throughout, grain and livestock continued to be exported from Ireland—the net caloric significance of which is debated, but which became a focus of later reckoning. British relief was governed by laissez-faire economics and providential theology; Charles Trevelyan, Assistant Secretary to the Treasury and the official who administered it, went so far as to regard the famine as a providential check on population.*

**史论** — 把爱尔兰大饥荒称作"天灾"，是它最需要被拆穿的说法。疫病只是扳机；真正的结构性原因，是使千百万人被绑定在一种作物上的单一栽培、是缺席地主与细碎租佃的殖民地土地制度、是把救济让位于市场教条的政策选择。约翰·米切尔"上帝降下疫病、英国制造饥荒"一语，正是这一"人祸"论最锋利的表达。不过史学也要求审慎：多数当代史家（如伍德姆-史密斯、Ó Gráda）虽严厉批评英国政策之失当与冷酷，却未必认同"蓄意灭绝"的指控——无能、教条与漠视，未必等于蓄谋。在图谱中，这条脉络因此以 causes 边接入其结构成因（单一栽培、殖民土地制度、放任政策），再以 inspires 边引出它所催生的回响：爱尔兰民族主义、土地同盟，与横跨大西洋的移民潮。
*To call the Great Famine an "act of God" is the label it most needs taken apart. The blight was only the trigger; the structural causes were the monoculture that bound millions to a single crop, the colonial land system of absentee landlords and subdivided tenancies, and the policy choice that subordinated relief to market doctrine. John Mitchel's line—"the Almighty sent the blight, but the English created the famine"—is the sharpest expression of this "man-made" reading. Yet history also demands care: most modern historians (Woodham-Smith, Ó Gráda) condemn the folly and coldness of British policy without necessarily endorsing the charge of deliberate extermination—incompetence, dogma, and indifference are not the same as intent. In the graph this thread therefore enters through causes edges to its structural origins (monoculture, the colonial land system, laissez-faire relief), and out through inspires edges to what it provoked: Irish nationalism, the Land League, and the great transatlantic emigration.*
