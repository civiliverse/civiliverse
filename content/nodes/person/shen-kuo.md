**卷首引文** — 「此物后必大行于世，自予始为之。」（论石油）——沈括《梦溪笔谈》卷二十四（约1088）
*"This substance will surely come into wide use in the world; the naming of it begins with me." (on petroleum) — Shen Kuo, Dream Pool Essays, c. 1088*

**定位句** — 一个把毕昇的泥活字、磁针的偏角和石油的未来都随手记下的人——幸亏他记了。
*The man who jotted down Bi Sheng's clay type, the needle's declination, and the future of petroleum alike—and lucky for us he did.*

**历史叙述** — 沈括字存中，北宋钱塘人，历任要职，曾参与王安石变法、主持司天监与边防。晚年隐居润州梦溪园，著《梦溪笔谈》二十六卷，内容自天文、数学、地质、物理、生物到工程、音乐，无所不包。正是在这部笔记里，他用数百字记下了布衣毕昇的胶泥活字——若无此记，这项发明将湮没无闻。他还首次记载磁针「常微偏东，不全南也」（磁偏角），比欧洲早约四百年；命名「石油」并预言其「后必大行于世」；由太行山崖间的螺蚌化石，推断此地曾为海滨、华北平原乃泥沙淤积而成。
*Shen Kuo (courtesy name Cunzhong) was a Qiantang man of the Northern Song who held high office, took part in Wang Anshi's reforms, and directed the Bureau of Astronomy and frontier defence. In retirement at the Dream Brook estate near Runzhou he wrote the twenty-six-chapter Dream Pool Essays, ranging over astronomy, mathematics, geology, physics, biology, engineering, and music. It was here, in a few hundred characters, that he recorded the commoner Bi Sheng's baked-clay type—without which the invention would have vanished unrecorded. He also first noted that the magnetic needle "always points slightly east, not due south" (declination), some four centuries before Europe; coined the term "petroleum" (shíyóu) and predicted it would "come into wide use"; and, from marine fossils in the cliffs of the Taihang mountains, inferred that the region had once been coast and that the North China Plain was built of silt.*

**史论** — 沈括的价值，恰在他多半不是「发明者」而是「记录者」。图谱把他与活字之间连一条 contributed 边，取的正是这一层——他对活字的贡献不是造，而是**记**：技术史里，一个把别人的发明认真写下来的人，其历史位置有时不亚于发明者本身。R2 曾把「唯一近世记录者」误挂在蔡伦名下（张冠李戴），R3 已改归沈括，正是为了让这条边名副其实。沈括也是宋代那种「通才官僚—博物学者」的标本：科学尚未分科，一个人可以既修历法、又察化石、还记活字——知识的边界，是后来才画上的。
*Shen Kuo's value lies precisely in his being, for the most part, not an "inventor" but a recorder. The graph joins him to movable type with a contributed edge in exactly this sense: his contribution was not to make the type but to write it down—in the history of technology, one who carefully records another's invention sometimes stands no lower than the inventor. R2 had mis-attached "sole near-contemporary recorder" to Cai Lun; R3 reassigns it to Shen Kuo, so the edge means what it says. Shen Kuo is also a specimen of the Song polymath-official: with science not yet partitioned, one man could reform calendars, read fossils, and record type alike—the borders of knowledge were drawn only later.*
