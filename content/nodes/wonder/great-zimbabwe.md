**卷首引文** — 「石垣之中，有一堡垒，砌石为之，不施灰泥……土人不知其为何人所建，亦不知其何时所建。」——巴罗斯《亚洲十年史》（1552）
*"Among these edifices there is one, built of stones of marvellous size, and there appears to be no mortar joining them … the natives of the country do not know how they were built, nor by whom." — João de Barros, Décadas da Ásia (1552)*

**定位句** — 关于这座城最著名的问题——「究竟是谁建的」——本身就是一件需要解释的历史事实。
*The most famous question about this city—who really built it—is itself a historical fact in need of explanation.*

**历史叙述** — 大津巴布韦坐落在今津巴布韦东南、林波波河与赞比西河之间的高地，海拔约一千一百米，是绍纳语「dzimba dza mabwe」（石头房子）之所指。营建自 11 世纪始于山丘一带，13 至 14 世纪达于鼎盛，形成山丘建筑群、大围场与谷地聚落三部分。其石工的特点是**干砌**：花岗岩沿天然节理剥离成板，修整为大小相近的块体，层层错缝叠砌，不用任何黏结材料，全靠形制与重量维持稳定；最精工的墙段砌缝齐整如线，顶部饰以人字纹（chevron）带。这种做法对石料的选择与修整要求极高，而对灰浆技术全无依赖——它不是「缺少灰浆的建筑」，而是另一套解决稳定问题的办法。经济上，此城据黄金产区与东海岸港口之间的通路：内陆金、象牙输往索法拉、基尔瓦，换回布匹、玻璃珠与瓷器；遗址所出中国青瓷、波斯釉陶与阿拉伯钱币，是这条商路的直接物证。15 世纪该城衰落，居民散去，政治中心北移至穆塔帕；葡萄牙人 16 世纪自沿海听说内陆有石城，巴罗斯的记载即出于这类传闻。19 世纪末，罗德斯的英国南非公司进入这一地区，随即出现一套说法：如此建筑非本地人所能为，必是腓尼基人或示巴女王的遗迹。1905 年考古学家兰德尔-麦基弗、1929 年凯顿-汤普森先后依层位与出土物断定其为中世纪非洲本土所建，学界自此定论；但罗德西亚政府直至 1970 年代仍要求官方出版物不得明确陈述其非洲起源，考古学者加莱克等因坚持相反结论而受压。1980 年新国家独立，以此城之名为国名。
*Great Zimbabwe stands on the plateau between the Limpopo and the Zambezi in the southeast of the present country, some eleven hundred metres above sea level, and is what the Shona words dzimba dza mabwe—houses of stone—refer to. Building began in the eleventh century on the hill, reached its height in the thirteenth and fourteenth, and comprises the Hill Complex, the Great Enclosure, and the settlement in the valley between. Its masonry is dry-laid: granite split along its natural sheeting into slabs, dressed to blocks of like size, and coursed with staggered joints without any binder, its stability resting on form and weight alone. The finest stretches are laid to a line and finished at the top with a chevron band. The method demands much in the selection and dressing of stone and nothing at all of mortar technology—this is not architecture lacking mortar but another solution to the problem of stability. Economically the city commanded the road between the goldfields and the eastern ports: gold and ivory went down to Sofala and Kilwa, and cloth, glass beads, and porcelain came back. The Chinese celadon, Persian glazed ware, and Arab coins from the site are direct evidence of that route. The city declined in the fifteenth century, its people dispersed, and the political centre moved north to Mutapa. Portuguese on the coast heard in the sixteenth century of a stone city inland, and Barros's notice comes from such reports. When Rhodes's British South Africa Company entered the region at the end of the nineteenth century, an account promptly appeared: such building was beyond local capacity and must be the work of Phoenicians or of the Queen of Sheba. David Randall-MacIver in 1905 and Gertrude Caton-Thompson in 1929 established from stratigraphy and finds that it was medieval and African, and scholarship has held so since; yet the Rhodesian government required into the 1970s that official publications not state its African origin plainly, and archaeologists such as Peter Garlake were pressed for maintaining the contrary. In 1980 the new state took the city's name for its own.*

**史论** — 这座城在科技史里的位置有两层，一层关于石头，一层关于关于石头的话。关于石头的一层不复杂：干砌石构是一套成熟的建造技术，它对材料学的要求（如何顺节理取板、如何控制块体的长厚比使墙体稳定）不低于任何用灰浆的做法，只是把稳定性问题交给了几何与重量而非化学。以「不会用灰浆」来评价它，如同以「不会用铁」来评价石器时代的燧石工艺——是拿别人没打算解决的问题去衡量别人解决了的问题。第二层更要紧。1891 年之后关于大津巴布韦的「谜」，不是史料不足造成的，而是被制造出来的：殖民统治需要一个前提——本地人没有建立复杂社会的能力——而这座城是这个前提的反证，于是它必须改姓。此后的发展给了科技史一份罕见的、可控的观察材料：同一批遗址、同一批出土物，在殖民政府的资助下得出一种结论，在独立的学术审查下得出另一种；而后者从 1905 年起就已经在证据上占尽优势，仍花了七十年才能被公开陈述。**这提醒我们，「证据充分」与「结论可说」是两件事，它们之间隔着一个通常不出现在方法论教科书里的变量。** 本图谱收录这一条，一半是为那道墙，一半是为这件事。
*The city's place in the history of technology has two layers, one concerning the stone and one concerning what has been said about the stone. The first is not complicated: dry-stone construction is a mature building technique whose demands on the properties of materials—how to take slabs along the sheeting, how to proportion blocks so that a wall stands—are no lighter than those of any mortared method; it simply refers the problem of stability to geometry and weight rather than to chemistry. To judge it by an inability to use mortar is like judging Stone Age flintwork by an inability to use iron: measuring what someone did solve against a problem they did not set. The second layer matters more. The mystery of Great Zimbabwe after 1891 was not created by a shortage of evidence but manufactured: colonial rule required a premise—that the local population was incapable of complex society—and this city was the premise's refutation, so it had to be renamed. What followed gave the history of technology a rare and almost controlled observation: the same site and the same finds yielded one conclusion under a colonial government's patronage and another under independent scholarly scrutiny, and the latter, which had the evidential advantage from 1905 onward, still took seventy years to become sayable in public. Sufficient evidence and a statable conclusion are two different things, and between them lies a variable that does not usually appear in textbooks of method. This graph includes the entry half for the wall and half for that.*
