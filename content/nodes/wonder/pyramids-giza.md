**卷首引文** — 「人惧时间，而时间惧金字塔。」——阿拉伯谚语
*"Man fears time, but time fears the pyramids." — Arabic proverb*

**定位句** — 世上关于它的疑问，多数问错了对象：难的不是那些石头，是那些人。
*Most of the questions asked about it are addressed to the wrong object: the difficulty was never the stones but the people.*

**历史叙述** — 吉萨三座金字塔属古王国第四王朝，依次为胡夫、哈夫拉、门卡乌拉所建，年代约在公元前 2600 至前 2500 年。胡夫金字塔底边长约 230 米，四边长度之差不足 20 厘米；底面近乎水平，全场高差约 2 厘米；四面正对四方，与真北的偏差不足四分之一度——这些精度以绳、桩、水槽与星象观测达成，是本工程最难解释的部分。石料的大宗为就地采掘的石灰岩，覆面用尼罗河东岸图拉的细石灰岩，王室墓室的花岗岩梁自八百公里外的阿斯旺运来。2013 年瓦迪贾夫红海沿岸出土的莎草纸中，有一位名叫莫勒尔的工头留下的工作日志：他率约四十人的队伍，以船往返图拉与吉萨，每趟数日，记录整齐如账。这份文书连同 1990 年代以来发掘的建造者聚落——面包房、酿酒作坊、鱼骨与牛骨堆、集体宿舍与监工住所，以及一处成规模的工匠墓地——共同勾勒出的图景是：一支由轮值农民与常备技工组成的队伍，由国家以面包、啤酒与肉食供养，工匠死后葬于王陵近旁。希罗多德在两千年后记下的「十万奴隶」，是传闻而非记录。
*The three pyramids at Giza belong to the Fourth Dynasty of the Old Kingdom, raised in turn for Khufu, Khafre, and Menkaure between about 2600 and 2500 BCE. Khufu's has a base some 230 metres to a side, its four sides differing by less than 20 centimetres; the base is level to about 2 centimetres across the whole field; and the sides face the cardinal points to within a quarter of a degree of true north—accuracies achieved with cord, stakes, water channels, and observation of the stars, and the hardest part of the work to account for. The bulk of the stone was limestone quarried on the spot, the casing fine limestone from Tura on the east bank, and the granite beams of the king's chamber came from Aswan, eight hundred kilometres upstream. Among the papyri found in 2013 at Wadi al-Jarf on the Red Sea is the working diary of an overseer named Merer, who led a gang of some forty men by boat between Tura and Giza, several days to the round trip, recorded as tidily as a ledger. That document, together with the builders' settlement excavated from the 1990s—bakeries, breweries, heaps of fish and cattle bone, dormitories and overseers' quarters, and a substantial cemetery of workmen—yields a picture of rotating peasant levies and a standing corps of skilled men, fed by the state on bread, beer, and meat, the craftsmen buried near the king they had built for. Herodotus's hundred thousand slaves, written down two millennia later, is hearsay and not record.*

**史论** — 金字塔在通俗文化里长期是一道「不可能的谜」，这份神秘感有其社会来源：它假定古人不可能做到这些，因而必须另找解释。但真正值得惊讶的地方，恰恰在证据最充分的那一面。搬起一块两吨半的石头，用坡道、木橇、水润与足够多的人，是个可解的问题；把两百多万块这样的石头在二十来年里逐块放到该在的位置，同时让上万人天天有饭吃、有工可分、有账可查、有人管——这才是那个真正惊人的成就。金字塔首先是一项**行政成就**：它需要测量与记账，需要把尼罗河的汛期与农闲编进工期，需要一套能把面包与啤酒准确送到每个工班手里的配给系统。莫勒尔的日志之所以珍贵，正因为它是这套系统的一页流水账——人类留下的最早的项目管理文书之一。第二层是关于「巨工」这一形态本身：从吉萨到大运河，凡集中动员的巨大工程，其技术遗产往往不在成品而在**过程**——测量、调度、后勤、记录，这些能力一旦建立便可挪作他用，而那座建筑物本身只是它们最显眼的副产品。第三层需要一点克制：把金字塔说成「奴隶的血泪」固然是错的，把它说成「劳动者的自豪」也未必对。轮值徭役是义务，不是选择；它的组织水平之高与它对个人的支配之深，本来就是同一件事的两面。
*In popular culture the pyramids have long been an impossible puzzle, and the mystery has a social source: it assumes the ancients cannot have done this, and so another explanation must be found. Yet what deserves astonishment lies on the best-evidenced side. Lifting a two-and-a-half-tonne block, given ramps, sledges, water, and enough people, is a solvable problem. Placing two million such blocks where each belongs across some twenty years, while ten thousand people eat daily, are assigned work, are accounted for, and are supervised—that is the astonishing achievement. The pyramid is first of all an administrative accomplishment: it required survey and bookkeeping, the fitting of work seasons to the Nile's flood and the farming year, and a ration system able to deliver bread and beer accurately to every gang. Merer's diary is precious exactly because it is one page of that system's running account—among the earliest documents of project management humanity has left. A second layer concerns the form of the mega-project itself: from Giza to the Grand Canal, the technical legacy of great mobilizations lies less in the product than in the process—survey, scheduling, logistics, record-keeping—capacities that, once built, can be turned to other ends, the monument being only their most conspicuous by-product. A third layer requires restraint. To call the pyramids the blood and tears of slaves is wrong; to call them the pride of labour may not be right either. A rotating levy is an obligation, not a choice, and the height of its organization and the depth of its command over individuals were always two sides of one thing.*
