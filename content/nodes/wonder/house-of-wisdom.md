**卷首引文** — 「伊玛目马蒙对科学的那份爱好……鼓励了我写下这部关于『还原与对消』的算书。」——花拉子米《代数学》献词（约820年）
*"That fondness for science by which God has distinguished the Imam al-Ma'mun… has encouraged me to compose a short work on al-jabr and al-muqābala." — al-Khwārizmī, dedication of the Algebra*

**定位句** — 一座把希腊、波斯、印度的学问译成阿拉伯文、再传回世界的翻译工坊——文明的中转站。
*A translation workshop that rendered Greek, Persian, and Indian learning into Arabic and passed it back to the world—a civilization's relay station.*

**历史叙述** — 8世纪末至9世纪，阿拔斯王朝定都巴格达，在哈里发（尤其马蒙，813–833在位）的赞助下，兴起一场规模空前的希腊—阿拉伯翻译运动。学者们把托勒密的天文、欧几里得的几何、盖伦的医学、亚里士多德的哲学，连同波斯、印度的星表与数码，系统译为阿拉伯文；「智慧宫」(Bayt al-Ḥikma) 一名，常用来统称这一藏书、翻译与研究的中心。这里不只是「保存」古典遗产的仓库——花拉子米在此立代数、算法（见 #25），侯奈因·伊本·伊斯哈格精译医典，天文学家校验托勒密、测量地球。它把三大文明的知识熔于一炉，再向东西两方传布。1258年，旭烈兀的蒙古大军攻陷巴格达，城破人亡，传说底格里斯河因投入的典籍而墨黑数日。
*From the late 8th into the 9th century the Abbasid caliphate, capital at Baghdad, sponsored—under caliphs, above all al-Ma'mun (r. 813–833)—a translation movement of unprecedented scale. Scholars rendered Ptolemy's astronomy, Euclid's geometry, Galen's medicine, and Aristotle's philosophy, together with Persian and Indian star-tables and numerals, systematically into Arabic; the name "House of Wisdom" (Bayt al-Ḥikma) is often used for this center of collection, translation, and research. It was no mere warehouse "preserving" the classical inheritance—al-Khwārizmī founded algebra and algorism here (see #25), Hunayn ibn Ishaq translated the medical canon with precision, and astronomers checked Ptolemy and measured the Earth. It fused the knowledge of three civilizations and passed it east and west. In 1258 Hülegü's Mongol army stormed Baghdad; the city fell, and legend says the Tigris ran black for days with the ink of books thrown in.*

**史论** — 智慧宫最该纠正的，是一种至今流行的辉格偏见：所谓「古典学问在欧洲沉睡千年，直到文艺复兴才被唤醒」。事实是，这份遗产从未沉睡——它在巴格达被译成阿拉伯文、被批注、被推进，再经安达卢斯与西西里的译场回流拉丁欧洲。没有这一环，就没有后来的「文艺复兴」。故图谱把智慧宫列入「知识奇观」群，与亚历山大图书馆、那烂陀、廷巴克图手稿群以 parallels 无向边相连——它们是不同文明在「汇聚、翻译、增益知识」上的彼此呼应。但也须存一分史学审慎：晚近研究（如 Gutas）提醒，「智慧宫」作为一座宏伟「学院」的形象，有后世浪漫化的成分——它更可能是图书馆与翻译活动的统称，而非一所建制学府。奇观的光环，也需要考据来擦拭。它的终局（1258）与那烂陀一样，是又一条被兵火截断的路；所幸此时知识已流布在外，未随一城俱焚。
*What the House of Wisdom most needs to correct is a still-popular Whig prejudice: that classical learning "slept in Europe for a thousand years until the Renaissance woke it." In fact the inheritance never slept—it was translated into Arabic in Baghdad, annotated, and advanced, then flowed back to Latin Europe through the translation centers of al-Andalus and Sicily. Without this link there would have been no later "Renaissance." The graph therefore places the House of Wisdom in the cluster of "knowledge wonders," joined by undirected parallels edges to the Library of Alexandria, Nalanda, and the Timbuktu manuscripts—different civilizations echoing one another in gathering, translating, and enlarging knowledge. Yet a measure of scholarly caution is due: recent work (e.g. Gutas) warns that the image of the "House of Wisdom" as a grand academy is partly a later romance—it was more likely a library and a body of translation activity than a chartered institution. A wonder's halo, too, must be wiped clean by source-criticism. Its end (1258), like Nalanda's, was another road cut by war and fire; mercifully, by then the knowledge had already spread beyond, and did not burn with the city.*
