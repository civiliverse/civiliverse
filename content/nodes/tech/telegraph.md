**卷首引文** — 「上帝创造了何等奇迹！」——莫尔斯1844年首份公开电文（语出《民数记》）
*"What hath God wrought!" — Morse's first public message, 1844 (Numbers 23:23)*

**定位句** — 在此之前，消息跑得再快也不过是一匹马；在此之后，消息跑在所有人前面。
*Before it, the fastest news went at the speed of a horse. After it, news went ahead of everyone.*

**历史叙述** — 1830年代，电磁学的实验成果具备了工程化的条件。1837年，库克与惠斯通在英国取得五针式电报专利并用于铁路信号；同年莫尔斯在美国提出单线加编码方案，1844年华盛顿至巴尔的摩线路开通，首份公开电文传遍报端。此后二十年，陆线沿铁路迅速铺开，海底电缆随之而来：1851年多佛—加莱，1858年首条跨大西洋电缆通报数周即告失效，1866年方告成功。到19世纪末，电缆网已覆盖各大洋，伦敦与孟买、上海之间的通信以小时计。后果四面展开：新闻业转向「快讯」体例，路透等通讯社兴起；商品与证券市场的价格差被迅速抹平；军队获得远程指挥能力；铁路为避免撞车而催生了统一时间，此后各国陆续采用标准时区。殖民行政亦大受其惠——1857年印度起义后，英国在南亚大举布线，电报成为帝国的神经系统。
*By the 1830s the experimental results of electromagnetism were ripe for engineering. In 1837 Cooke and Wheatstone patented a five-needle telegraph in Britain and applied it to railway signalling; the same year Morse proposed a single wire with a code, and the Washington–Baltimore line opened in 1844 with a first message that ran in every newspaper. Over the next twenty years land lines spread along the railways and submarine cables followed: Dover–Calais in 1851, a transatlantic cable in 1858 that failed within weeks, and a durable one in 1866. By the end of the century cables crossed every ocean and London could reach Bombay or Shanghai within hours. Consequences opened on every side: journalism turned to the bulletin and agencies such as Reuters arose; price differentials in commodity and securities markets were rapidly arbitraged away; armies gained command at a distance; and railways, needing to avoid collisions, forced the adoption of uniform time, from which standard time zones followed. Colonial administration benefited above all—after the rebellion of 1857 Britain wired South Asia extensively, and the telegraph became the nervous system of empire.*

**史论** — 电报常被称作「维多利亚时代的互联网」，这个比喻抓住了它的速度，却容易漏掉它的方向性。信息流向哪里、为谁所用，取决于线路由谁铺设。1876—78年的印度大饥荒是最沉重的例证：当时南亚已有相当密度的铁路与电报网，理论上足以及时调粮；实际发生的却是电报把各地粮价迅速联通，铁路把粮食高效地从歉收区运往出口港——技术使市场一体化，而一体化的市场把粮食送到了出得起价的地方，不是最饿的地方。戴维斯据此提出「维多利亚晚期大屠杀」的论断，也有史家认为其对气候（厄尔尼诺）与殖民政策的权重分配可再商榷。本图谱不为这场争论下判决，但坚持写出那个不容回避的事实：**同一批技术，既可以用来救灾，也可以用来把粮食运走，选择权不在技术手里。**
*The telegraph is often called the Victorian internet, a comparison that captures its speed but tends to miss its directionality. Where information flows, and for whom, depends on who lays the wire. The Indian famines of 1876–78 are the gravest illustration: South Asia by then had a considerable density of railway and telegraph, in principle enough to move relief grain in time. What happened instead was that the telegraph rapidly linked prices across regions and the railway carried grain efficiently from failing districts to the export ports—technology integrated the market, and the integrated market sent grain where the money was, not where the hunger was. Mike Davis argued from this to his thesis of "late Victorian holocausts"; other historians hold that his weighting of climate (El Niño) against colonial policy is open to revision. This graph does not adjudicate the dispute, but it insists on stating the fact that cannot be evaded: the same technologies can be used to bring relief or to carry the grain away, and the choice does not lie with the technology.*
