**卷首引文** — 「他们自幼习于乘马，故不良于行；其国人皆在马上。」——阿米阿努斯·马尔切利努斯记匈人（约 390 年）
*"They are so unused to walking, being always on horseback, that they can scarcely stand upon the ground; a whole people on horses." — Ammianus Marcellinus on the Huns (c. 390 CE)*

**定位句** — 这个题目在过去十年里被重写了一遍——不是因为挖到了新的马骨，而是因为学会了读旧马骨里的基因。
*The subject has been rewritten in the past decade—not because new horse bones were dug up, but because we learned to read the genes in the old ones.*

**历史叙述** — 马的驯化长期以哈萨克斯坦北部的博泰文化为起点。该遗址（约前 3500）的动物骨骼中马骨占九成以上，部分马齿的前臼齿边缘有疑似衔具造成的磨损，陶片脂类分析检出马乳成分——三条证据合起来，博泰一度被写进教科书作为最早的驯马。2018 年高尼茨等人对博泰马的古基因组测序给出了一个出人意料的结果：这些马属于普氏野马的谱系，而普氏野马此前一直被当作从未被驯化的真正野马。也就是说，博泰人确实在管理马群、挤马乳、或许也骑马，但那批马并不是今日家马的祖先——它们是一次**独立的、后来中断的**驯化。2021 年利夫拉多与奥兰多等人测定了两百余份跨欧亚的古马基因组，把现代家马谱系（记为 DOM2）定位在黑海—里海草原的伏尔加—顿河一带，约前 2200 年前后开始扩张，并在此后两三百年内迅速取代欧亚各地原有的马群——其扩张速度与辐条轮战车的传播、若干印欧语支的分化在时间上高度重合。此后马的用途分为两支：驾车与骑乘。辛塔什塔文化的辐条轮战车（约前 2000）是驾车一支的最早确证；骑乘作为成建制的军事力量则要到前一千纪，随复合弓与骑射战术成型，才在草原上定形——此后两千年，欧亚农耕文明与草原骑马者的关系，成为大陆历史的一条主轴。
*The domestication of the horse was long dated from the Botai culture of northern Kazakhstan. At that site (c. 3500 BCE) horse bone makes up more than nine-tenths of the fauna, some premolars show wear plausibly caused by a bit, and lipid analysis of potsherds detects mare's milk—three lines that together put Botai into the textbooks as the earliest domestication. In 2018 Charleen Gaunitz and colleagues sequenced ancient genomes from Botai horses with an unexpected result: they belong to the Przewalski lineage, which had until then been taken for the one truly wild horse never domesticated. The Botai people were indeed managing herds, milking mares, and perhaps riding—but those horses are not ancestral to ours. Theirs was a separate domestication that later lapsed. In 2021 Pablo Librado, Ludovic Orlando and their collaborators sequenced more than two hundred ancient horse genomes across Eurasia and placed the modern lineage, DOM2, in the Volga–Don region of the Black Sea–Caspian steppe, expanding from about 2200 BCE and replacing the region's other horse populations within a few centuries—an expansion closely coincident in time with the spread of the spoked-wheel chariot and with the divergence of several Indo-European branches. The horse's uses then divided in two, driving and riding. The spoked-wheel chariots of the Sintashta culture (c. 2000 BCE) are the earliest secure evidence of driving; riding as an organized military arm took form on the steppe only in the first millennium BCE, with the composite bow and the tactics of mounted archery. For the two thousand years that followed, the relation between the farming civilizations of Eurasia and its mounted peoples was one of the continent's principal axes.*

**史论** — 这一条最值得留在读者心里的，或许不是马，而是**这个题目本身在十年间被改写的方式**。博泰说不是被新发现的遗址推翻的，也不是被更好的论证推翻的——推翻它的是一项新的读取技术。古 DNA 测序在 2010 年代成熟之后，考古学的许多长期争论从「哪一种解释更合理」变成了「基因组怎么说」，而基因组给出的答案往往不合任何一方的预期。这带来两个后果，都值得科技史注意。其一是**史学的方法依赖**：一门学科能问的问题，受限于它能读取的痕迹；痕迹的可读性一变，问题清单随之重排——这与 `germ-theory` 条所记「一门学科接受什么算作证据本身有历史」是同一件事的另一面。其二是**谦逊的必要**：博泰说曾有三条独立证据支持、写进教科书数十年，然后错了。它不是粗率的推断，而是当时能做到的最好推断。**教科书上的定论与「已知的最好答案」之间，永远差着下一项读取技术。** 至于马本身，其历史意义常被压缩为军事优势，但更深的一层在半径：一个人一日可及之地由二三十公里扩到上百公里，意味着牧群可以在更大的草场间轮转，消息可以在几日内跨越数百公里，一支队伍可以出现在没有道路通达的地方。草原游牧作为一种生活方式、而非仅是一种经济，是在马之后才成立的。
*What may deserve to stay with a reader here is not the horse but the manner in which the subject was rewritten within a decade. The Botai account was not overturned by a newly found site, nor by a better argument, but by a new technique of reading. As ancient-DNA sequencing matured through the 2010s, many long-standing archaeological disputes turned from which interpretation is more reasonable into what the genomes say—and the genomes have often answered as no party expected. Two consequences follow, both worth the historian of technology's notice. The first is method dependence: the questions a discipline can ask are bounded by the traces it can read, and when the legibility of traces changes, the list of questions is reordered. This is the other face of what germ-theory records, that what a discipline accepts as evidence has a history of its own. The second is the necessity of modesty. The Botai account had three independent lines of support and stood in textbooks for decades, and it was wrong—not through careless inference, but as the best inference then available. Between a settled conclusion in a textbook and the best answer known, there is always the next technique of reading. As for the horse, its historical weight is usually compressed into military advantage, when the deeper layer is radius. A day's reach extended from twenty or thirty kilometres to a hundred and more, which meant herds could rotate among distant pastures, news could cross hundreds of kilometres in days, and a force could appear where no road ran. Pastoral nomadism as a way of life, and not merely as an economy, exists only after the horse.*
