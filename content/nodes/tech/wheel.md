**卷首引文** — 「赫柏迅速给车装上两轮，青铜的，八辐的，转在两端的铁轴上。」——荷马《伊利亚特》第五卷
*"Hebe in speed set about the chariot the curved wheels, eight-spoked and brazen, with an axle of iron both ways." — Homer, Iliad V*

**定位句** — 轮子的难处不在轮子。会做圆盘的人很多；能让圆盘绕着一根不动的轴稳稳转上千里的人，要晚几千年才出现。
*The hard part of the wheel is not the wheel. Many peoples could cut a disc; making one turn steadily about a motionless axle for a thousand miles took several thousand years more.*

**历史叙述** — 最早的轮不用于走路，而用于制陶：约公元前4000年的美索不达米亚已有转盘成形的陶器。用于运输的轮出现于约前3500年，证据在三处几乎同时浮现——波兰布罗诺西采出土陶罐上刻着四轮车、两河流域乌鲁克的泥板上有车形符号、斯洛文尼亚卢布尔雅那沼泽出土了一只完整木轮（约前3200年）。孰先孰后至今未决。真正的技术门槛在轮轴：轮孔与轴须同时圆整，轴要足够细以减小摩擦、又足够粗以承重，这套公差要求超出了此前的木工。约前2000年，辐条轮在西亚出现，车重骤减，战车随之成为青铜时代晚期的主战兵器。此后两千年，轮的形制变化有限，直到近代把它按在一条铁轨上——轮缘代替了驾驭，方向不再由人给出，而由轨道给出。中美洲的情形另成一格：瓦哈卡等地出土过带轮轴的小陶像，年代在公元数百年间，说明轮轴原理并非未知；但当地没有可供驾驭的大型役畜，地形多山多林，既有的人力背运与独木舟体系运转有效，轮车因而没有出现。
*The earliest wheels did not travel: by about 4000 BCE Mesopotamian potters were shaping vessels on a turning disc. Wheels for transport appear about 3500 BCE, and the evidence surfaces in three places almost at once—a four-wheeled wagon incised on a pot at Bronocice in Poland, wagon signs on tablets at Uruk, and a complete wooden wheel from the Ljubljana Marshes in Slovenia (c. 3200 BCE). Which came first is still undecided. The real threshold was the axle: hub and shaft must both be true, and the shaft thin enough to reduce friction yet thick enough to bear load—a tolerance beyond earlier carpentry. Around 2000 BCE the spoked wheel appeared in western Asia; vehicle weight fell sharply and the chariot became the decisive arm of the late Bronze Age. For the two millennia after, the wheel changed little, until the modern age set it on an iron rail—where a flange replaced the driver, and direction was given no longer by a hand but by the track. Mesoamerica forms a case of its own: small ceramic figures with wheels and axles, of the first centuries CE, have been excavated at Oaxaca and elsewhere, so the principle was known. But there were no large draught animals to harness, the terrain was mountain and forest, and the existing system of human porterage and canoes worked. The wheeled vehicle did not follow.*

**史论** — 中美洲的有轮陶偶是科技史上最好的一堂课。按目的论的读法，这是一次「差一步」的遗憾：他们已经摸到门口，却没走进去。但把条件摆齐就会看到，没走进去才是合理的——没有牛马可驾，在山地与雨林中开路的成本高过背运，一条独木舟顶得上几十个脚夫。轮车在旧大陆之所以扩散，不是因为轮比脚好，而是因为那里恰好同时具备可驯的大型役畜、可通行的平原与足够大的货运量。**技术不会因为「更先进」而被采用，只会因为在当地更划算而被采用。** 反过来说，玛雅人把轮装在祭祀用的小陶狗身上、印加人在没有轮的情况下修出两万五千公里的道路网，都不是失败的证据，而是不同约束下的合理解。今天我们把这类现象叫作「未采用」，那个「未」字里藏着的，仍是一条我们尚未完全戒掉的进步阶梯。
*The wheeled figurines of Mesoamerica are the best lesson in the history of technology. Read teleologically they are a near miss: they reached the door and did not go through. Set out the conditions and not going through is the reasonable outcome—no cattle or horses to harness, a cost of cutting road through mountain and rainforest higher than carrying, one canoe worth dozens of porters. Wheeled transport spread in the Old World not because the wheel beats the foot but because that world happened to hold, together, domesticable draught animals, passable plains, and freight enough to matter. A technology is not adopted for being more advanced; it is adopted for paying better where it stands. Conversely, the Maya fitting wheels to small ceramic dogs and the Inca building twenty-five thousand kilometres of road with no wheel at all are not evidence of failure but sound answers under different constraints. We call such cases "non-adoption," and inside that "non" there is still a ladder of progress we have not quite given up.*
