**卷首引文** — 「岛屿不动，动的是船与星。」——卡罗琳群岛航海传统之教法（口传的通行转述）
*"The island does not move; the canoe and the stars do." — a teaching of the Caroline Islands tradition*

**定位句** — 没有海图、没有罗盘、没有六分仪，却把地球上最大的一片水域住满了人。
*No chart, no compass, no sextant—and yet the largest body of water on earth was settled.*

**历史叙述** — 太平洋岛民的定向依靠一整套相互校验的自然线索。核心是「星罗盘」：把地平圈按若干恒星升落的方位分格，夜航时依次以将升或将落之星保持航向。白昼则读涌浪——远方常年风系造成的长浪穿过岛屿会折射、交叉，老练的领航者能凭船体的起伏辨出数种涌浪的叠加方向，甚至躺在独木舟中以身体感知。近岛的迹象另成一套：晨出暮归的鲣鸟与燕鸥、礁湖反射在云底的绿光、漂来的枝叶、水色与温度的变化，可把一个岛的「有效直径」由几公里扩展至数十公里。船体则是双体舟与舷外浮杆，兼顾载重与抗浪。20世纪中叶，夏普提出岛屿是被风暴偶然漂送的人所定居，一时颇有影响；1976年，波利尼西亚航海学会以复原双体舟 Hōkūleʻa 号，由密克罗尼西亚萨塔瓦尔岛的领航者 Mau Piailug 不用仪器领航，自夏威夷航抵塔希提，此说遂告瓦解。此后的计算机漂流模拟亦表明，纯漂流几乎不可能抵达夏威夷、新西兰与复活节岛。
*Pacific navigation rested on a set of mutually checking natural cues. At its core is the "star compass": the horizon divided into houses by the rising and setting points of selected stars, the canoe held on course by taking successive stars as they rise or set. By day one reads swells—long waves raised by distant prevailing winds refract and cross around islands, and a practised navigator can distinguish several superimposed swell trains from the motion of the hull, lying down in the canoe to feel them. Signs of nearby land form another set: boobies and terns that fly out at dawn and back at dusk, the green underglow of a lagoon on the cloud base, drifting vegetation, changes in the colour and temperature of the water—together these expand an island's effective diameter from a few kilometres to several tens. The vessels were double-hulled canoes and outriggers, balancing capacity against seaworthiness. In the mid-twentieth century Andrew Sharp argued that the islands had been settled by people blown there accidentally, a view that carried some weight; in 1976 the Polynesian Voyaging Society sailed the reconstructed double-hulled Hōkūleʻa from Hawaiʻi to Tahiti, navigated without instruments by Mau Piailug of Satawal in Micronesia, and the argument collapsed. Later computer simulations of drift confirmed that pure drift could scarcely reach Hawaiʻi, Aotearoa, or Rapa Nui.*

**史论** — 这一条挑战的是「知识必须写下来才算知识」的成见。波利尼西亚航海术没有一个字的文献，它存在于口诀、吟唱、师徒同舟的数十年训练与身体记忆之中，其精度却足以在数千公里外找到一个直径十公里的岛。把它称作「原始导航」是一种范畴错误：它不是仪器导航的低配版，而是另一套完备的解法——以人体为传感器，以整片海洋为参照系。夏普假说的兴衰也值得记取：那不是一个恶意的理论，而是一个从「他们没有仪器，故不可能有意远航」的前提出发的推论。**当我们看不见某种知识的载体时，很容易断定它不存在。**推翻它的不是论战，而是一次真正的航行。
*This entry challenges the assumption that knowledge counts as knowledge only when written. Polynesian navigation has not a word of documentation; it lives in mnemonic chant, in decades of apprenticeship aboard the same canoe, in bodily memory—and its precision sufficed to find an island ten kilometres across from thousands of kilometres away. To call it "primitive navigation" is a category error: it is not a stripped-down version of instrument navigation but a complete alternative solution, using the human body as sensor and the whole ocean as frame of reference. The rise and fall of Sharp's hypothesis is worth remembering too. It was not a malicious theory but an inference from the premise that people without instruments could not have voyaged deliberately. When we cannot see the vehicle of a body of knowledge, it is easy to conclude that the knowledge is not there. What refuted it was not argument but an actual voyage.*
