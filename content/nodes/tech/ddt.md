**卷首引文** — 「DDT 对我好得很！」——宾索特化工公司广告，《时代》1947年
*"DDT is good for me-e-e!" — Pennsalt Chemicals advertisement, Time, 1947*

**定位句** — 一种先获诺贝尔奖、后被公约点名的杀虫剂——「奇迹」与「代价」是同一种分子。
*An insecticide that first won a Nobel Prize and was later named in a treaty—the "miracle" and the "cost" are one and the same molecule.*

**历史叙述** — DDT（双对氯苯基三氯乙烷）1874年由奥特马·蔡德勒首次合成，但其杀虫性直到1939年才被瑞士嘉基公司的化学家保罗·米勒发现，米勒因此获1948年诺贝尔生理学或医学奖。二战后半程，DDT 被大规模用于为士兵与平民灭虱灭蚊——1943至44年那不勒斯斑疹伤寒的扑灭是其成名之役——并成为战后疟疾防治的主力。它廉价、高效、对哺乳动物急性毒性低，一时被奉为化学的胜利，喷洒于农田、市镇乃至儿童身上。然而正是它的持久与脂溶，使其沿食物链层层富集。1962年蕾切尔·卡森《寂静的春天》敲响警钟；1972年美国环保署（EPA）署长拉克尔肖斯宣布取消其绝大部分用途；2001年《斯德哥尔摩公约》将其列入持久性有机污染物，仅保留疾病媒介防治的公共卫生豁免。
*DDT (dichloro-diphenyl-trichloroethane) was first synthesized by Othmar Zeidler in 1874, but its insecticidal power was not discovered until 1939, by the chemist Paul Müller of the Swiss firm Geigy, who received the 1948 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for it. In the second half of WWII, DDT was used on a massive scale to delouse and protect soldiers and civilians—its suppression of the Naples typhus outbreak of 1943–44 made its name—and it became the mainstay of postwar malaria control. Cheap, effective, and of low acute toxicity to mammals, it was hailed as a triumph of chemistry and sprayed over fields, towns, and even children. Yet its very persistence and fat-solubility made it accumulate up the food chain. In 1962 Rachel Carson's Silent Spring sounded the alarm; in 1972 the US EPA administrator William Ruckelshaus cancelled most of its uses; and in 2001 the Stockholm Convention listed it as a persistent organic pollutant, keeping only a public-health exemption for disease-vector control.*

**史论** — 1947年那句"DDT 对我好得很"的广告，今天读来像一则黑色寓言的开篇——但把 DDT 简化为一桩"化学骗局"，同样是一种辉格式的偷懒。它的历史是一道尚未算清的权衡账：一端是被它从疟疾、斑疹伤寒中救回的千百万条命，另一端是被它压垮的猛禽种群与被它改写的生态认知。1972年的禁令并非故事的道德终点——DDT 至今仍在部分疟疾高发区依公约豁免使用，而"禁 DDT 害死了多少人"也已成为一个被政治化的反问。本节点因此以 causes 边接入它引发的生态危机，以 informs 边接入疟疾防治理念（技术实践反哺理念），两条边都必须在场：技术的账，从来不是单栏记的。
*That 1947 slogan, "DDT is good for me," now reads like the opening of a dark fable—but to reduce DDT to a "chemical fraud" is its own kind of Whiggish shortcut. Its history is a ledger not yet balanced: on one side, the millions saved from malaria and typhus; on the other, the raptor populations it crashed and the ecological awareness it rewrote. The 1972 ban was not the moral end of the story—DDT is still used under treaty exemption in some high-malaria regions, and "how many died because DDT was banned" has itself become a politicized rhetorical question. This node therefore joins the graph through a causes edge to the ecological crisis it triggered and an informs edge to the idea of malaria control; both edges must be present, because a technology's account is never kept in a single column.*
