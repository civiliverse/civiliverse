**卷首引文** — 「我所设想的工具，如同铜版印刷：可印出许多张彼此难辨的图。」——惠特尼致沃尔科特，1799年
*"The tools which I contemplate are similar to an engraving on copper plate from which may be taken a great number of impressions perceptibly alike." — Eli Whitney, 1799*

**定位句** — 一项以「零件都一样」为目标的技术，其最著名的演示是一场表演。
*A technology whose goal was making parts identical; its most famous demonstration was a performance.*

**历史叙述** — 手工时代的火枪，每一支的零件都由同一名工匠配到同一支枪上，坏了须重新锉配。1780年代，法国枪械师布朗展示了可互换的燧发枪机：来访的美国公使杰斐逊亲见其从若干堆零件中随取组装，深为触动并去信国内。法国大革命打断了这条线索。1798年，惠特尼以互换零件为承诺取得美国政府一万支步枪的合同，并于1801年在国会前演示装配——20世纪的档案研究（尤以梅里特·罗·史密斯为代表）表明，那些零件事先编号配对，演示是精心安排的；他的交货亦一再延期。真正的推进来自联邦兵工厂：斯普林菲尔德与哈珀斯费里在数十年间持续投入量具、夹具、样板与铣床，把「合格」从工匠的手感转移到硬性的检验规上，约1840年代方达成实用意义上的互换。此法随后离开兵工厂，进入缝纫机、钟表、农机与自行车制造，1850年代英国考察团称之为「美国制造体系」，20世纪初经福特与流水线接合，成为大规模生产的基础。
*In the age of handcraft each musket's parts were fitted by one smith to that one musket, and a breakage meant filing a new part to fit. In the 1780s the French gunsmith Honoré Blanc demonstrated interchangeable flintlock mechanisms: Thomas Jefferson, then minister in Paris, watched him assemble locks from bins of parts taken at random, was much struck, and wrote home about it. The French Revolution cut that thread. In 1798 Eli Whitney won a federal contract for ten thousand muskets on a promise of interchangeability and in 1801 assembled arms before Congress—archival work in the twentieth century, notably by Merritt Roe Smith, showed the parts had been numbered and matched in advance, the demonstration carefully staged; his deliveries ran years late. The real advance came from the federal armories: Springfield and Harpers Ferry invested for decades in gauges, jigs, patterns, and milling machines, shifting the definition of "correct" from the craftsman's feel to hard inspection gauges, and reached practical interchangeability around the 1840s. The method then left the armories for sewing machines, clocks, agricultural implements, and bicycles; British commissioners in the 1850s named it "the American system of manufactures," and in the early twentieth century, joined to Ford's moving line, it became the basis of mass production.*

**史论** — 这一条的价值在于它拆穿了两种迷思。其一是英雄发明家：惠特尼的演示是表演，而使互换成真的是兵工厂里数十年不起眼的量具与工序改良——**技术史的主角常常没有名字**。其二是「技术即物」：可更换零件几乎不含新的物理原理，它的实质是一套关于精度、检验与标准的**组织安排**。因此它被放在「经济·制度·治理」为主领域之下，而非材料或机械。它的深层后果同样在组织层面：装配从此可由非熟练工完成，工匠的知识被转移到机器与量具之中——这是劳动技能被「去技能化」的开端，卢德派所抗争的正是同一进程的另一副面孔（见「卢德运动」）。
*This entry punctures two myths. The first is the heroic inventor: Whitney's demonstration was theatre, and what made interchangeability real was decades of unglamorous gauge-and-process work inside the armories—the protagonists of technical history frequently have no names. The second is that technology is a thing: interchangeable parts involve almost no new physical principle, and consist essentially in an organizational settlement about precision, inspection, and standards. Hence the node's primary domain is economy-governance rather than materials or mechanics. Its deeper consequences are organizational as well: assembly could now be done by the unskilled, as the craftsman's knowledge migrated into machines and gauges. This is the beginning of deskilling, and what the Luddites fought was another face of the same process (see "the Luddites").*
