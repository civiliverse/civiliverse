**卷首引文** — 「我通晓晦涩的苏美尔文、艰深难解的阿卡德文，也能读懂大洪水之前的石刻。」——亚述王亚述巴尼拔宫廷泥板题记（约前7世纪）
*"I have read cunningly written Sumerian and obscure Akkadian, and deciphered stones inscribed from before the Flood." — Colophon of King Ashurbanipal, 7th c. BCE*

**定位句** — 人类最早的文字，最初不是为了写诗或祷告，而是为了记账。
*Humanity's earliest writing was invented not for poetry or prayer, but for keeping accounts.*

**历史叙述** — 约公元前3400至3200年，两河流域南部的苏美尔城邦发展出楔形文字。书吏以削尖的芦苇笔在湿泥板上压印，笔画呈楔形，故名。最早的泥板几乎全是账目：某神庙收进多少大麦、某人交割多少绵羊——文字的第一桩差事是记账，而非叙事。此后数百年间，符号由图形趋于抽象，可记录苏美尔语的音节，进而被阿卡德、巴比伦、亚述、赫梯、埃兰等多种语言借用，用于法典（《汉谟拉比法典》）、史诗（《吉尔伽美什》）、天文与外交（阿马尔那泥板）。它通行近三千年，最晚的可纪年泥板约在公元75年。19世纪，格罗特芬德与罗林森借波斯贝希斯敦三语摩崖，逐步破译了这套沉睡的文字。
*Around 3400–3200 BCE the Sumerian city-states of southern Mesopotamia developed cuneiform. Scribes pressed a cut reed stylus into wet clay, leaving the wedge-shaped strokes that give the script its name. The earliest tablets are almost all accounts: so much barley received by a temple, so many sheep delivered by a man—writing's first task was bookkeeping, not narrative. Over centuries the signs grew from pictures to abstractions able to record the syllables of Sumerian, and were then borrowed for Akkadian, Babylonian, Assyrian, Hittite, Elamite and more—for law codes (Hammurabi), epic (Gilgamesh), astronomy, and diplomacy (the Amarna letters). It served for nearly three thousand years, its last datable tablet around 75 CE. In the 19th century Grotefend and Rawlinson, working from the trilingual Behistun inscription in Persia, gradually deciphered the sleeping script.*

**史论** — 楔形文字戳破了一个浪漫想象：文字并非诞生于诗人或祭司的灵感，而是长在**官僚与仓库**的账目里。它最早的用户是清点大麦绵羊的书吏——正如印刷最早的大用户是佛教徒（见「雕版印刷」），技术的第一推动力常来自平淡的实务。图谱把「楔形文字」列为 tech（物质性的书写系统），与作为 idea 的「腓尼基字母」（书写的抽象原理）区分开。它也是「独立起源」的一例：两河的楔形、尼罗河的圣书字、黄河的甲骨、中美洲的玛雅字——文字至少被人类独立发明了数次，没有唯一的源头。这正是反辉格史观所珍视的：书写不是从某一处「扩散」到全世界的礼物。
*Cuneiform punctures a romantic notion: writing was born not of poets' or priests' inspiration but in the ledgers of bureaucracy and the storehouse. Its first users were scribes counting barley and sheep—just as print's first heavy users were Buddhists (see "Woodblock Printing"), a technology's first push often comes from mundane practice. The graph lists "cuneiform" as a tech (a material writing system), distinct from the idea-node "Phoenician alphabet" (the abstract principle of writing). It is also a case of independent origin: Mesopotamian cuneiform, Nile hieroglyphs, Yellow-River oracle bones, Mesoamerican Maya glyphs—writing was invented by humanity at least several times over, with no single source. This is what anti-Whig history prizes: writing was not one place's gift, "diffused" to the rest of the world.*
