**卷首引文** — 「他们的肉身以黄玉米与白玉米造成。」——《波波尔·乌》（基切玛雅创世书）
*"Of yellow maize and of white maize their flesh was made." — Popol Vuh (K'iche' Maya)*

**定位句** — 美洲把一种作物送给了世界，却没能把与它配套的那道工序一起送出去。
*The Americas gave the world a crop, and failed to send the process that goes with it.*

**历史叙述** — 玉米的野生祖先是大刍草，一种穗上只结五到十二粒、外裹硬壳的墨西哥禾草。2002年的多位点微卫星分析把驯化指向墨西哥西南巴尔萨斯河谷的一个大刍草亚种；该流域的洞穴遗址出土了约公元前7000年的玉米淀粉粒与植硅体。此后数千年，穗轴由易碎变为不裂、颖壳退化、粒数与粒径大幅增加——这是一次形态变化极大的驯化，大到19世纪的植物学家一度不相信二者有亲缘。约前2000年，玉米已在中美洲成为主粮，并北传至西南普韦布洛、南抵安第斯边缘。与选育同行的是一道化学工序：把玉米粒放入石灰水或草木灰水中煮浸，外皮易脱、面团易黏，同时释放出玉米中以结合态存在、人体无法吸收的烟酸。1492年后玉米迅速传遍旧大陆——耐旱、单产高、适应广，在意大利北部、巴尔干、非洲与中国山地成为穷人主粮；碱法蒸煮却没有跟着传。18世纪起，糙皮病在阿斯图里亚斯、伦巴第与罗马尼亚流行；1902至1940年代的美国南方，此病造成十万人以上死亡，直到戈德伯格证明它是膳食缺乏而非传染病。
*Maize's wild ancestor is teosinte, a Mexican grass whose ear carries five to twelve hard-cased kernels. Multilocus microsatellite analysis in 2002 placed domestication in a teosinte subspecies of the Balsas valley in southwestern Mexico, where cave sites have yielded maize starch grains and phytoliths of about 7000 BCE. Over the following millennia the cob ceased to shatter, the glumes reduced, and kernel number and size increased greatly—a morphological change so large that nineteenth-century botanists doubted the two plants were related. By about 2000 BCE maize was a staple in Mesoamerica, spreading north to the Puebloan southwest and south to the fringes of the Andes. Alongside the breeding went a chemical process: soaking and boiling the grain in lime or wood-ash water loosens the pericarp and makes the dough cohere, while freeing the niacin that maize holds in a bound form the human gut cannot take up. After 1492 maize crossed rapidly to the Old World—drought-tolerant, high-yielding, widely adaptable, it became the food of the poor in northern Italy, the Balkans, Africa, and the uplands of China. Nixtamalization did not cross with it. From the eighteenth century pellagra spread through Asturias, Lombardy, and Romania; in the American South between 1902 and the 1940s it killed more than a hundred thousand people, until Joseph Goldberger showed it to be a deficiency of diet and not an infection.*

**史论** — 玉米的故事常被讲成物种交换的成功案例：一种美洲作物养活了旧大陆的人口增长。这是真的，但只讲了一半。作物可以装船，工序不行。碱法蒸煮在中美洲已行之三千余年，它是一套嵌在日常里的知识——泡多久、用哪种灰、手感如何算好——从未写成配方，也没有理由被写成配方，因为在那里它从不需要解释。跨洋而来的人取走了看得见的那一半：种子、产量、耐旱。两百年后，人们才在实验室里重新发现那道工序为什么要紧。**技术转移的失败，通常不在于难的那部分没学会，而在于简单到没人想起要说的那部分没被带走。** 这一点至今不过时：任何一次「引进先进技术」，真正丢掉的往往正是当地人觉得不值一提的操作常识。另一层则关乎归因的分寸：糙皮病并非只由缺工序造成，贫困与膳食单一是同等重要的条件——一道工序解释不了一场流行病，正如一粒种子解释不了一次人口增长。
*Maize is often told as a success of exchange: an American crop that fed Old World population growth. True, and half the story. A crop can be shipped; a process cannot. Nixtamalization had been practised in Mesoamerica for three thousand years as knowledge embedded in daily life—how long to soak, which ash, what the dough should feel like—never written as a recipe, and with no reason to be, because where it was practised it never needed explaining. Those who crossed the ocean carried off the visible half: seed, yield, drought tolerance. Two centuries passed before the laboratory rediscovered why the process mattered. Failures of technology transfer are usually not about the difficult part going unlearned, but about the part too simple to mention going uncarried. The point has not dated: in any importation of "advanced technology," what is most often lost is the operational common sense the locals thought not worth saying. A second layer concerns proportion in attribution: pellagra was not caused by a missing process alone—poverty and dietary monotony were conditions of equal weight. One process no more explains an epidemic than one seed explains a population.*
